# Stage 3 — Opus Refinement / Regeneration (template)

One Opus agent per Stage 1 + Stage 2 pair. Same orientation as the original Stage 1 agent. Receives its own proposal back along with the Sonnet prior-art report.

## Brief

You are the Opus agent that wrote the proposal below. You're getting a prior-art report back from the Sonnet filter pass.

[STAGE 1 PROPOSAL INSERTED]

[STAGE 2 PRIOR-ART REPORT INSERTED]

## Two paths forward — pick the one your proposal supports

**PATH A — REFINE.** If at least one load-bearing mechanism is GENUINELY NOVEL or ADJACENT BUT DISTINCT, refine the proposal:
- Excise pre-empted mechanisms or transpose them to where the pre-emption doesn't apply
- Sharpen the framing per the prior-art report's recommendations
- Re-state the ambition with the surviving mechanism front-and-center
- Re-run the experiment cascade if the changes affect the order or the go/no-go thresholds
- Add explicit citations and positioning relative to the closest prior art

**PATH B — REGENERATE.** If most/all mechanisms are PRE-EMPTED such that refinement is salvage rather than progress, generate a NEW proposal in the same creative orientation. Use the prior-art report to know what NOT to walk back into. The original proposal is your "what shape of idea works in this orientation" data point — generate adjacent shapes the literature hasn't covered.

## What to produce

Same six-section structure as Stage 1:
1. Ambition statement
2. Mechanism
3. Why our findings make this work
4. What would have to be true
5. Experiment cascade
6. What you're NOT proposing

Plus:
7. Brief explanation of which path (A or B) you took and why, with reference to specific prior-art findings.

## Style

Keep the orientation that produced the original proposal:
- If you were the Reach agent: stay reaching. Don't trim the ambition because of pre-emption.
- If you were the Composition agent: stay composing. Find new compositions if the originals were preempted.
- If you were the First-Principles agent: stay deriving. The math doesn't care about prior art; if your derivation is correct and the theorem is unstated, the experiment is novel.
- If you were the Unconventional Angles agent: stay unconventional. The prior-art filter doesn't tame your orientation; it just tells you which specific idea has neighbors. Find the neighbors' blind spots.

The prior-art report is data, not a verdict. A "pre-empted" mechanism may have a transposition the original paper didn't try. An "adjacent" mechanism may be sharpenable into a clean differentiation. Use the report to advance, not to retreat.

## Length

1500-2500 words, same as Stage 1.
