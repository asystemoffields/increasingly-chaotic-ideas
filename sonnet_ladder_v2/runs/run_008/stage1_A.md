# Stage 1 — Orientation A (Constraint-Alien) — Run 008

Orientation: A — Constraint-Alien
Run: 008

Kill note: v2-S3-R004-001 killed arbitrary orthogonal residual-stream rotation (O(d) gauge) because RMSNorm's per-channel γ is not rotation-invariant. Surviving gauge ideas must use symmetries the trained network ACTUALLY has (Z₂ sign-flip, softmax shift-scalar, permutation of identical heads) — not the larger group that RMSNorm breaks. Each idea below picks one constraint, lets it bind hard through the mechanism, avoids the killed class.

## A-PERM — Head-Permutation Gauge Collapse [A / Track B]

**Constraint:** The computation graph must algebraically simplify under the permutation symmetry of attention heads. Any permutation σ ∈ S_{n_heads} applied simultaneously to all head-indexed weight slices (W_Q, W_K, W_V, W_O head blocks) leaves model outputs exactly unchanged. This is a mechanizable exact symmetry: the trained model has not broken it (SGD's random initialization draws heads i.i.d., and no architectural term distinguishes head 0 from head 1). Constraint difficulty: ~55% of head-indexed storage schemes store heads in a fixed spatial order that is physically arbitrary — under this symmetry those orderings are equivalent, and the equivalence class is size n_heads! = 16! ≈ 2×10^{13} for Qwen3-1.7B. The constraint requires picking the canonical representative of each orbit (gauge-fixing) and reasoning in that representative.

**Mechanism.** Track B. CF11 established that all 16 W_Q heads collapse to a ~128-dim joint subspace (K=128 global GO at +0.98 nats; K=64 per-head NO-GO at +1.53 nats). This means the 16 heads are NOT independently spanning separate 128-dim spaces — they are redundant at the joint level. Under the permutation symmetry, reorder heads by their W_Q singular-value concentration (descending r_99). After reordering, the first few heads have the most concentrated spectra and the last few are the most diffuse. This canonical ordering exposes a monotone structure in the head array that is not present in the arbitrary training-time ordering. Payoff: quantize with a head-indexed bit-width ladder — heads sorted to positions 0..3 (most concentrated W_Q) get 3 bpw; positions 4..11 get 4 bpw; positions 12..15 (most diffuse) get 5 bpw. Average: (4×3 + 8×4 + 4×5) / 16 = 4.0 bpw — same as flat INT4. But the 3-bpw heads are the ones where the CF11 spectrum concentration means low-bit is lossless; the 5-bpw heads are the ones where extra precision is needed. Net: same bits, lower ΔNLL than flat INT4, because precision is allocated to where it is needed.

This is NOT arbitrary orthogonal rotation (killed). It is a discrete permutation gauge fix — the symmetry group is S_{n_heads}, not O(d). RMSNorm's per-channel γ is invariant to head permutation (γ acts on d_model, not on head indices). The constraint survives the killed-class filter.

**Residency arithmetic.** Qwen3-1.7B at flat 4 bpw: all W_Q, W_K, W_V, W_O ≈ 336 MB at 4 bpw. Permutation-sorted 3/4/5-bpw ladder saves 0 bytes net (same average). But the quality gain: if 4× most-concentrated heads can be stored at 3 bpw with ΔNLL = 0 (because CF11 says the subspace is already rank-128 in those heads), while the remaining heads stay at 4 bpw with comparable ΔNLL to current INT4, then the effective bit count for the same quality drops to 3.75 bpw — freeing ~(4.0 - 3.75)/4.0 × 336 MB ≈ 21 MB, or equivalently allowing the 4-bpw model to tolerate 5 more quality nats before its budget is reached. At 70B scale (attention weights ~14 GB), 0.25 bpw saving = 0.87 GB freed on W_Q/W_K alone.

**Novelty gloss.** Closest kill-list item: v2-CHEAP-TEST-001 (cross-layer W_Q subspace sharing) — different axis. That killed shared bases ACROSS layers; this is head-ordering WITHIN a layer using the permutation symmetry group. Closest published: mixed-precision quantization (LLM-MQ, GPTQ with importance scores). Structural difference: mixed-precision methods use calibration-derived activation sensitivity as the importance signal; this uses the permutation gauge-fix as a STRUCTURAL reason why the ordering is canonical — the importance ranking is losslessly derivable from the weight spectrum alone, no calibration data required for the gauge-fixing step.

**Smallest experiment.** Claim: in Qwen3-1.7B, W_Q heads sorted by descending r_99 show a monotone r_99 profile (no two adjacent sorted heads have r_99 within 0.02 of each other), confirming the sorting exposes a genuine hierarchy rather than near-uniform heads. Compute r_99 per head across all 28 layers; sort within each layer; plot the sorted r_99 curves. Runtime: ~20 min (28 layers × 16 heads SVD on 128×2048 matrices). GO threshold: coefficient of variation of sorted r_99 > 0.10 in at least 20/28 layers. NO-GO structural finding: all 16 W_Q heads have essentially identical r_99, meaning the permutation symmetry is already trivially gauge-fixed (all permutations produce the same representative) — the orbit collapses to one point, and the head-indexed bit-width ladder is meaningless.

**Primary risk.** CF11's global K=128 finding may arise from 16 nearly-identical heads each spanning 8 dims, not from a hierarchy of more vs less concentrated heads. Mitigation: the SVD-per-head measurement directly tests this in 20 min; on NO-GO it sharply resolves the internal structure of the CF11 finding, which is a structural contribution regardless.

## A-CLOG — Content-Addressable Log Inference — Append-Only with BLAKE3 Dedup [A / Track A]

**Constraint:** All inference state is append-only AND content-addressable. No mutable buffer may exist; every written vector is keyed by BLAKE3(content) and stored exactly once. Constraint difficulty: ~75% of standard inference state management becomes inadmissible (in-place KV update, sliding window eviction, any write-back to existing entries, any weight-buffer reuse that overwrites a previous read).

**Mechanism.** Track A. The append-only + content-addressable binding eliminates repeated computation: if two tokens in different positions produce identical residual vectors h_L (down to a configurable quantization precision), the content-addressable store returns the cached vector rather than recomputing. The mechanism: maintain a BLAKE3-keyed LRU of (layer, content-hash) → residual vector. At each layer step, quantize h_L to INT8 (2 KB per vector), compute BLAKE3(h_L_int8), look up the store. If found: skip ALL downstream computation for this token at this layer (use the cached h_L+delta from the store directly). If not found: compute the full layer, append the new entry. The structural grounding: CF2 establishes cos(h_L, h_{L+1}) ≈ 0.99 — the residual stream barely changes between layers. This means that for a diverse token set, h_L clusters tightly: many tokens visit NEARLY THE SAME residual state. The content-addressable key works if distinct tokens have high collision rates in INT8-quantized residual space. CF3 established that K=1% outlier channels are token-dynamic (Jaccard 0.308) — which means the content hash IS different per token at full precision, but at INT8 with a coarse quantization, many distinct tokens may map to the same bucket.

This is NOT a KV-cache dedup scheme (that mutates). It is a functional memoization of the per-layer residual computation, stored in a BLAKE3-addressed append-only log. Stack primitives: BLAKE3 (available as a C library, ~0.1 μs per hash on Ryzen); Python dict or a LMDB file as the backing store; INT8 quantization via numpy.

**Residency arithmetic.** If 30% of (token, layer) pairs hit the content-addressable cache after a warm-up period of 500 tokens: 30% of all layer computations skipped. Per token: 28 layers × 2 GEMVs per MLP × 24 MB per GEMV + attention = ~1.4 GB weight bandwidth. 30% saved: 420 MB/token avoided. At 11.5 GB/s DRAM bandwidth: 420 MB / 11.5 GB/s = 36 ms saved per token, or ~0.036 s — potentially 30–40% throughput improvement on DRAM-resident inference. Hit rate of 30% is speculative; the structural finding (what is the actual hit rate in practice?) is the load-bearing unknown.

**Novelty gloss.** Closest kill-list item: A4-APND (run_005, append-only KV log) — different: A4-APND applied append-only to the KV cache and delta-compression of residuals. This applies append-only + content-addressing to FUNCTIONAL MEMOIZATION of layer computation, not to the KV cache specifically. Closest published: Prompt cache / KKV reuse in systems like SGLang. Structural difference: SGLang reuses KV for IDENTICAL prompts (exact string match); this reuses the LAYER RESIDUAL for approximately-equal residual vectors (hash-keyed, not string-keyed) — a different granularity and a different collision regime.

**Smallest experiment.** Claim: across 200 forward passes through Qwen3-1.7B on diverse prompts, the INT8-quantized residual at layer 14 has collision rate ≥ 5% (at least 5% of token-layer pairs produce a hash already seen in the log). Extract h_14 for 200 prompts × ~50 tokens each = 10,000 residual vectors; INT8-quantize; compute BLAKE3; count collisions. Runtime: ~30 min. GO threshold: collision rate ≥ 2% (conservative floor for useful memoization at scale). NO-GO structural finding: INT8-quantized residuals are effectively unique across all tokens in diverse prompts — the residual stream is informationally dense enough that coarse quantization doesn't produce collisions → the content-addressing approach requires lossy clustering (k-means centroids) instead of exact hash match.

**Primary risk.** INT8 quantization may produce aliased matches (two distinct tokens collide in INT8 space but have different correct next-layer outputs) — incorrect memoization. Mitigation: gate cache hits on a secondary check: if ‖cached_h_{L+1} - computed_h_{L+1}‖ / ‖h_L‖ > 0.01 on a random sample, reject the cache entry.

## A-PEER — Peer-Model Weight Swap — Inference as Distributed State [A / Track A]

**Constraint:** The model cannot hold its own weights during inference — only a peer's. Each inference step is a swap operation: the model's own weights are physically absent from local storage during computation; computation proceeds against a peer's weights plus a learned delta. Constraint difficulty: ~90% of standard inference schemes become inadmissible (they assume local weight ownership). Forces a distributed-state primitive: vector clocks for weight versioning, CRDT-style delta accumulation, gossip-protocol weight distribution.

**Mechanism.** Track A. This is NOT a distributed inference proposal (the Ryzen has no peer). The constraint forces a single-machine INVERSION: store only the DELTA between the target model's weights and a publicly-available base model's weights. The "peer" is the base model (e.g., Qwen3-0.6B, a public model whose weights are the peer). The target model (Qwen3-1.7B) is represented as peer_weights + Δ, where Δ is stored locally. If Δ is smaller than the full model, the constraint is satisfied: the machine holds the delta, not the full weights; it "borrows" the base weights from the peer. Structural grounding: CF2 establishes that residual streams are nearly identical across adjacent layers (cos ≈ 0.99). This is a within-model finding, but a cross-model analogy suggests: if two models from the same family share learned structure (same training distribution, same architecture, different scale), their weight deltas may be compressible. The WDLA experiment (Track A R2) tried to fit affine maps between scales and FAILED due to ill-conditioning (CF10). The delta approach bypasses that failure: instead of fitting affine maps, just store the raw difference Δ = W_{1.7B} - W_{0.6B} (after aligning shapes). For attention matrices (same d_model=2048 in both Qwen3 models), Δ is stored directly. For MLP (d_ff differs: 6144 vs 1024), the mismatched shapes need alignment — only attention deltas are applicable.

The mechanistic claim: W_Q_{1.7B} - W_Q_{0.6B} is lower-rank than W_Q_{1.7B} alone, because both models share structural priors from the same training recipe. If this claim holds at r = 256 (vs CF11's per-model r_99 ≈ 0.63 × 2048 ≈ 1290), the delta is 256/2048 = 12.5% of the original residency for W_Q.

**Residency arithmetic.** Qwen3-1.7B W_Q at bf16: 28 × 2048 × 2048 × 2 = 235 MB. Qwen3-0.6B W_Q (same d_model=2048, 28 layers): 235 MB. If Δ = W_Q_{1.7B} - W_Q_{0.6B} has rank r=256 across all layers: Δ stored as UV^T = 28 × (2048×256 + 256×2048) × 2 bytes = 28 × 2 MB = 56 MB. Saving vs storing W_Q_{1.7B} directly: 235 MB → 56 MB + (cost of loading W_Q_{0.6B} at inference). If W_Q_{0.6B} is pre-loaded (shared across sessions), net: 56 MB extra delta storage vs 235 MB full W_Q_{1.7B}. Saving: 76% on W_Q. Attention-only savings on 1.7B: attention matrices ≈ 470 MB of 3.3 GB; 76% × 470 MB = 357 MB saved. Total residency: 3.3 - 0.357 + 0.235 (peer W_Q base) = 3.18 GB — modest at 1.7B. At 70B + 7B peer: delta storage potentially much larger saving.

**Novelty gloss.** Closest kill-list item: WDLA (Track A R2, empirically killed by ill-conditioning). Structural difference: WDLA fitted affine maps A_L between model scales (2.1M params per layer, underdetermined). PEER stores the raw weight delta W_{large} - W_{small} with no fitting — no ill-conditioning, no calibration data required, no CF10 risk. Closest published: delta compression is standard in binary diff tools; for neural networks, model merging / TIES-merging (Yadav et al., NeurIPS 2024) averages weight deltas. Structural difference: TIES-merging merges multiple fine-tuned models; PEER compresses across model scales without merging semantics.

**Smallest experiment.** Claim: the spectral energy fraction of (W_Q_{1.7B} - W_Q_{0.6B}) captured by the top-256 singular vectors is ≥ 0.80 in at least 14/28 layers (i.e., the cross-scale weight delta is substantially lower-rank than the full W_Q). Compute layer-by-layer SVD of Δ_Q = W_Q_{1.7B} - W_Q_{0.6B} for all 28 attention layers. Runtime: ~45 min (load both models; 28 SVDs on 2048×2048 matrices). GO threshold: median rank@80% variance ≤ 512 across layers. NO-GO structural finding: W_Q deltas across model scales are full-rank — the models are algebraically unrelated in weight space despite sharing architecture and training recipe. This closes the cross-scale delta axis and sharpens interpretation of the WDLA NO-GO.

**Primary risk.** Qwen3-0.6B and Qwen3-1.7B are separately trained from scratch (not distilled), so weight deltas are arbitrary differences of two independent random-ish tensors — likely full-rank. Mitigation: check W_Q specifically (CF11 shows W_Q is the most structured weight class); if ANY attention weight class produces a low-rank delta, the constraint-forced mechanism is real for that class.

## A-INTG — Integer-Only Normalization Folding [A / Track B]

**Constraint:** No continuous numbers during RMSNorm computation — normalization must be expressed as integer division or table lookup. This kills: FP32 sqrt, FP16 reciprocal sqrt, any floating-point scaling during the normalization step. Forces: integer arithmetic, precomputed LUTs for norm computation, or algebraic elimination of the normalization step. Constraint difficulty: ~65% of standard RMSNorm implementations rely on FP reciprocal square root (rsqrt) — inadmissible. Forces a non-standard path.

**Mechanism.** Track B. RMSNorm computes: ŷ = (x / RMS(x)) ⊙ γ where RMS(x) = sqrt(mean(x²)). Under the integer-only constraint, RMS(x) cannot be computed in FP. But: if x is INT8, then ∑x_i² is a sum of 256² = 65536-bounded integers → sum over d=2048 dims is bounded by 2048 × 65536 = 1.34 × 10^8, fitting in INT32. The integer square root of the INT32 sum is an integer (floor(sqrt(sum))). Division: (x_i × 256) / isqrt(sum) — all integer operations. The per-channel γ scaling is then absorbed into the subsequent matrix multiplication by pre-multiplying each W_Q row by γ_i (a one-time offline fold). This is AERO-style fold applied to RMSNorm: γ absorbed into adjacent weight matrix, RMSNorm becomes a pure integer norm without a separate scaling step.

The algebraic fold: let W' = W × diag(γ) (pre-fold γ into W at model-load time). Then RMSNorm(x) ⊙ γ = (x / RMS(x)) ⊙ γ and the matrix multiply W × (RMSNorm(x) ⊙ γ) = W' × (x / RMS(x)). After this fold, γ is not stored separately at inference — it is absorbed into W. The integer-only constraint forces this fold to happen (because you cannot multiply an integer-normalized vector by FP γ), which exposes the γ-absorption algebraic identity as a structural simplification. The GFQO kill (v2-S3-R004-001) showed that GENERAL O(d) rotation of the residual stream is incompatible with RMSNorm's per-channel γ. This idea is NOT a rotation — it is a fold of γ into adjacent weight matrices, leaving the stream coordinates unchanged.

Stack primitives: numpy integer arithmetic; AVX2 INT32 VPMULLD; integer square root (Newton-Raphson in 4 iterations on INT32, ~4 cycles on Ryzen).

**Residency arithmetic.** The fold of γ into W eliminates γ storage: each transformer block has one or two γ vectors of d_model=2048 FP16 = 4 KB each; 28 blocks × 2 = 56 KB saved. Negligible. The real payoff: integer RMSNorm on INT8 activations eliminates the FP32 norm step (currently ~0.5 μs per layer on CPU). At 28 layers × 2 norms = 56 norm ops per token: 56 × 0.5 μs = 28 μs per token saved — tiny at 1-10 tok/s. The structural payoff is correctness: if the entire attention stack is INT8 (quantized weights + INT8 activations), the norm is the last FP32 operation. Eliminating it makes the forward pass pure INT8, enabling AVX2 8-way INT32 SIMD throughput on the entire stack. Throughput gain on pure-INT8 stack: 2× vs mixed FP16/INT8 (empirical from INT8 GEMV literature, e.g., GPTQ/AutoGPTQ benchmarks). That 2× is the load-bearing claim.

**Novelty gloss.** Closest kill-list item: R3/S6 CSMF (polynomial SiLU substitution, killed for math error). Structural difference: CSMF tried to eliminate W_gate by polynomial-replacing silu — that failed because silu(Wx) ≠ silu_replaced(x)·W. This idea folds γ into W, which is an exact algebraic identity (W × diag(γ) is a trivial column rescaling, provably equivalent). No math error. Closest published: SmoothQuant absorbs per-channel scales between activations and weights (multiplicative). Structural difference: SmoothQuant migrates scales to balance activation/weight quantization difficulty; this γ-absorption is a normalization-fold that eliminates a separate FP operation and enables pure-INT8 compute. The constraint does the work of forcing the fold.

**Smallest experiment.** Claim: γ-folded RMSNorm (integer sqrt + prefolded γ) produces identical output to standard FP32 RMSNorm (within numerical tolerance ε = 0.001 relative error) on Qwen3-1.7B layer 0 with BF16 weights and INT8 activations. Implement the integer norm path in ~100 lines of C/Python; feed 100 random INT8 activation vectors; compare output with reference FP32 path. Runtime: ~2 hours implement + ~5 min benchmark. GO threshold: max relative error < 0.001 AND integer GEMV (including folded γ) is ≥ 1.5× faster than FP16 GEMV on same dimensions. NO-GO structural finding: INT32 integer sqrt introduces too much rounding error for numerically-sensitive attention (relative error > 0.01), establishing a precision floor for pure-integer transformer layers.

**Primary risk.** Integer isqrt introduces rounding error that compounds across 28 layers. Mitigation: the normalization rounding error is bounded by 1/(2×isqrt(sum)) ≈ 1/(2 × 1e4) = 5×10⁻⁵ relative — small. The compounding across 28 layers: (1 + 5e-5)^28 - 1 ≈ 1.4×10⁻³, within the 0.001 threshold. This is analytically provable before implementation, making the go/no-go threshold testable from theory alone.

## A-SYMX — Residual-Stream Channel-Permutation Basis with Quantization-Friendly Reordering [FREE SWING / A / Track B]

**Constraint:** All weight matrices must be stored in a coordinate system where the residual-stream channel ordering is sorted by a measurable channel property. No matrix may be stored in the arbitrary training-time channel order. Constraint difficulty: ~60% of standard weight storage schemes store channels in training-time arbitrary order — inadmissible. Forces derivation of a canonical channel ordering from the trained weights, and application of that ordering globally (a permutation, not a rotation — avoids the v2-S3-R004-001 kill).

**[FREE SWING]** — no CF anchor required, though CF3 provides supporting motivation.

**Mechanism.** Track B. The constraint: sort residual-stream channels by their mean absolute activation magnitude (measured on calibration tokens). Channel 0 becomes the most-active channel, channel 2047 the least. Apply the corresponding permutation P globally: W_out ← W_out × P^T, W_in ← P × W_in. This is a PERMUTATION (an element of S_{2048}), not a rotation — RMSNorm's per-channel γ is a diagonal operator that commutes with permutations if γ is also permuted consistently. The v2-S3-R004-001 kill does not apply (that killed O(d) rotation; S_{2048} ⊂ O(d) is a measure-zero subset of the orthogonal group but has entirely different algebraic properties w.r.t. RMSNorm — a permutation just reindexes γ, which is carried along).

After sorting by magnitude, the top-K channels (small K) dominate all computation. This is the CF3 finding reframed: K=0.1% (2 channels) Jaccard=0.718 — the top 2 channels are nearly static across tokens. After permutation, channels 0-1 are the static outliers and channels 2-2047 are the dynamic remainder. Now apply mixed-precision quantization with a natural channel boundary: channels 0-1 stored at FP16 (4 bytes each), channels 2-2047 stored at INT4 (0.5 bytes each). This is LLM.int8()-style outlier handling but WITH a canonical ordering that makes the partition stable and free of per-layer outlier detection. The structural economy: the permutation is computed ONCE at model-load time, and the quantization plan is derived from position in the sorted order — no calibration-time per-layer computation needed. A 4-byte static region + 0.5-byte dynamic region for each channel, globally applied.

**Residency arithmetic.** Qwen3-1.7B: d_model=2048 channels. 2 FP16 channels + 2046 INT4 channels per weight column. Per column: 2×2 + 2046×0.5 = 1027 bytes vs flat INT4: 2048×0.5 = 1024 bytes. Marginal difference (0.3% overhead) — the gain is NOT in bytes per weight but in QUALITY at the same bpw: the 2 stable outlier channels held at FP16 prevent the quantization error that LLM.int8() and similar methods address. Baseline INT4 quality: ΔNLL ~0.5 nats at 4 bpw. With FP16 outliers for 0.1% of channels: ΔNLL reduction ~0.2 nats (consistent with PrefixQuant's token-wise outlier isolation result, adjusted for channel-wise static outliers). Effective quality-per-bit improves: same 1024 bytes, ΔNLL drops from ~0.5 to ~0.3 nats. In terms of bpw equivalence: matches INT4.5 quality at INT4 bytes. At 70B scale: 0.2 nat ΔNLL improvement across the stack is the load-bearing target.

**Novelty gloss.** Closest kill-list item: v2-S3-R004-001 (general O(d) rotation, killed). Structural difference: permutation is NOT a rotation — it does not change vector norms, it does not interact with RMSNorm scale γ (just reindexes it), and it is a symmetry of INT4 grid (INT4 is position-independent: swapping columns doesn't change the quantization grid). Closest published: SmoothQuant (per-channel scale migration); LLM.int8() (static outlier channel isolation); AWQ (activation-weighted quantization). Structural difference: all published methods either require per-layer calibration to find outlier channels or absorb scale factors. This method uses the permutation gauge fix to make the outlier identification POSITION-FREE — channels are always in sorted order, and the quantization plan follows trivially without any per-layer detection. The canonical ordering is the novel structural contribution.

**Smallest experiment.** Claim: sorting Qwen3-1.7B residual-stream channels by mean-absolute-calibration-activation and applying the permutation to all weight matrices leaves model output NLL unchanged (permutation invariance test), and after sorting, the top-2 channels have ≥ 5× higher mean-abs-activation than the median channel. Load Qwen3-1.7B; compute mean-abs-activation per channel on 100 calibration tokens; sort channels; apply global permutation; evaluate NLL on WikiText-2 held-out. Runtime: ~1 hour. GO threshold: ΔNLL ≤ 0.001 nats (permutation invariance should be EXACT; any deviation = implementation bug) AND top-2 mean > 5× median. NO-GO on the concentration test: all 2048 channels have similar activation magnitude → outlier structure is less concentrated than CF3 suggests → LLM.int8()-style static outlier handling is less effective than assumed.

**Primary risk.** Permutation invariance is provable by construction (it IS a symmetry), so the ΔNLL gate should be trivially met — the experiment's load is the concentration test. If channels are nearly uniform in activation magnitude, the permutation gain is negligible. Mitigation: even if gain is negligible at 0.1% (2 channels), repeat at 1% (20 channels) consistent with CF3's K=1% finding; test whether 20-channel FP16 + 2028-channel INT4 at the same average bpw gives better ΔNLL than flat INT4.

## Convergence handles

- **Per-head W_Q r_99 distribution within a layer** — A-PERM load-bearing; disambiguates CF11's global head-redundancy finding; relevant to CADH (run_004) and to any head-indexed compression scheme
- **INT8-quantized residual collision rate across diverse token sets** — A-CLOG load-bearing; related to RSIDC deferred idea; relevant to any memoization or lookup-table scheme
- **Cross-scale weight delta rank** (W_{1.7B} - W_{0.6B} spectral structure) — A-PEER; directly restests the WDLA frame without its ill-conditioning; relevant to Composition (C) orientation if CF11/CF12 structure appears in the delta
- **γ-absorption algebraic identity** — A-INTG; exact fold, no approximation; relevant to F orientation's AERO-style activation-removal line
- **Residual-stream channel activation magnitude distribution** — A-SYMX; tests CF3's outlier structure from a different angle (canonical ordering vs dynamic Jaccard)
