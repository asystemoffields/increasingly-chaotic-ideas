## F1-A — Cross-Layer Query-Subspace Gauge Collapse (CQSGC)

**Track A** — arch-transposition: the W_Q computation graph collapses across layers into a single shared linear operator.

CF11 established that 16 query heads span a ~128-dimensional subspace globally (head-redundancy at 16:1 ratio, ΔNLL=+0.98 at K=128). The question left open is whether this subspace is *also* shared across the 28 layers or is merely each-layer-local. If all 28 layers' W_Q matrices (each 2048×2048) share a common dominant subspace of dimension D*, then:

Let Q_stacked = [W_Q^(1); W_Q^(2); ...; W_Q^(28)] ∈ R^{28×2048 × 2048}. Treat this as a mode-1 tensor unfolding. Define U ∈ R^{2048 × D*} as the shared right singular space. If ‖W_Q^(ℓ) U U^T - W_Q^(ℓ)‖_F / ‖W_Q^(ℓ)‖_F < ε for all ℓ with D* << 2048, then inference can proceed by: (1) projecting x into D*-dim coordinates once at the start of each block, and (2) applying a cheap 16-head × (D*/16)-dim attention in the projected space. This collapses 28 separate W_Q GEMVs into 28 projections that share most of their work. The "gauge" framing: the trained W_Q residual stream has a rotational gauge freedom (rotating all heads' Q-coordinates simultaneously does not change attention scores if K and V are co-rotated). The cross-layer shared subspace *is* the gauge-fixed coordinate system — the one the trained model implicitly uses.

**Residency arithmetic.** W_Q per layer: 2048×2048 = 4M params × 28 layers = 112M params = 224 MB at BF16. At D*=128: shared U at 2048×128=262K params (0.5 MB), per-layer coefficients W_Q^(ℓ) U at 2048×128=262K params × 28=7.3M params=14.7 MB. Total W_Q residency: ~15 MB vs ~224 MB. That is a 15× reduction in W_Q weight residency. On Qwen3-1.7B (total ~3.2 GB BF16), attention weights are ~896 MB (W_Q+W_K+W_V+W_O); W_Q alone is ~224 MB. Freeing 209 MB on a 7.28 GiB target is modest but clean. For 70B class where attention weight residency scales ~40× larger (~9 GB), a 15× W_Q reduction saves ~8.4 GB — this is material. ΔNLL at the calibration scale is bounded by CF11 (+0.98 at K=128 within-layer); cross-layer sharing, if it holds, comes for free because U is a Frobenius-optimal basis for the union of layer subspaces.

**Novelty gloss.** Closest kill-list item: CF11-motivated MLA-style joint Q-K projection — that's a within-layer head-sharing mechanism (open, not killed). CQSGC is different: it asks whether the shared subspace is *cross-layer*, not just cross-head. Closest published method: GQA/MQA/MLA share K/V heads across layers during training; they do NOT share Q projections. A3 (arXiv:2505.12942) post-training attention-logit minimization shares heads within a layer. Cross-layer Q subspace sharing post-training is distinct: the object is a single U that serves all 28 layers' queries simultaneously, derivable from PCA of the Q weight stack without retraining.

**Smallest experiment.** Claim: all 28 W_Q matrices share a common right subspace such that var@D*=128 across the stacked SVD exceeds 0.80. Procedure: stack W_Q^(ℓ) for all ℓ (each row-normalized), compute SVD of the 28×2048 × 2048 mode-1 unfolding via incremental SVD (nunmpy.linalg.svd on the covariance), measure cumulative variance at D* ∈ {64,128,256,512}. Runtime: ~15 minutes on Ryzen 5 7530U (28 matrix-matrix products, each 2048×2048, then one SVD of a 57344×2048 matrix). GO threshold: var@128 > 0.80. NO-GO threshold: var@128 < 0.50. NO-GO finding: establishes that W_Q layers are topologically independent (no shared subspace), which motivates instead a within-layer head-shared factorization path (already confirmed by CF11 to yield +0.98 ΔNLL at K=128).

**Primary risk.** Cross-layer Q subspace concentration may be much weaker than within-layer head redundancy — the two compressive effects may be orthogonal and the cross-layer test may come back at var@128 ≈ 0.3, well below GO. Mitigation: the experiment is pure measurement with no implementation cost; failure produces a confirmed bound on cross-layer Q structure that narrows the subsequent architecture-space.

## F2-B — Attention-Weight Joint Diagonalization Gauge Fix (AJDGF)

**Track B** — compression: bytes-per-weight reduction by identifying a simultaneous change of basis that jointly diagonalizes W_Q^(ℓ) and W_K^(ℓ) within each attention head.

W_Q^(ℓ) and W_K^(ℓ) together define the attention kernel at layer ℓ via A = softmax(Q K^T / √d_k) where Q = x W_Q, K = x W_K. The attention scores depend only on the operator M^(ℓ) = W_Q^(ℓ) W_K^(ℓ)^T ∈ R^{d×d}. This means (W_Q, W_K) and (W_Q Σ, W_K Σ^{-T}) for any invertible Σ ∈ R^{d_k×d_k} yield identical attention scores — a gauge freedom in the head-coordinate space. A first-principles question: is there a Σ* such that W_Q^(ℓ) Σ* is low-rank (say at rank r << d_k) AND W_K^(ℓ) Σ^{-T}* is also low-rank? This is the simultaneous low-rank problem. CF11 shows W_Q has r_99/d = 0.63 and W_K has r_99/d = 0.79 independently. Independent low-rank compressions (truncate W_Q and W_K separately) do NOT exploit the gauge freedom — they are just separate SVDs. AJDGF uses the gauge: find Σ* = argmin rank(W_Q Σ) + rank(W_K Σ^{-T}) subject to ‖M - W_Q Σ (W_K Σ^{-T})^T‖_F < δ. The solution is related to the CCA (Canonical Correlation Analysis) factorization of (W_Q, W_K): the CCA canonical directions are exactly the directions where the two matrices can be jointly compressed. Under the CCA gauge, both W_Q and W_K are block-diagonal, and low-rank truncation of both at rank r matches the joint operator M up to CCA-rank-r error rather than independent Frobenius error.

**Residency arithmetic.** Per-head: W_Q ∈ R^{128×2048}, W_K ∈ R^{128×2048} (16 heads in Qwen3-1.7B, d_head=128). At r=64 (CCA-rank): W_Q → U_Q Σ_Q (64×2048 + 64×128), W_K → U_K Σ_K (64×2048 + 64×128). Storage: 2×(64×2048 + 64×128) = 2×(131072+8192) = 278KB per head at BF16, vs 2×(128×2048)=524KB. Saving: ~47% per head. Over all 16 heads × 28 layers: 16×28 = 448 heads; saving 0.47×0.5×224 MB ≈ 53 MB for W_Q alone; 53+67=120 MB total for (W_Q, W_K) vs 448 MB original — saving ~73% of (W_Q, W_K) residency. On the 7.28 GiB target: 330 MB freed at Qwen3-1.7B scale; at 70B: proportional scaling gives ~1.9 GB freed in (W_Q, W_K). ΔNLL bound: since the gauge freedom is exact (M is preserved exactly when no truncation occurs), quality cost is determined by rank of the CCA truncation. CF11's +0.20 ΔNLL at W_Q K=512 (global) provides a loose upper bound at comparable compression ratios.

**Novelty gloss.** Closest kill-list item: CF11 MLA-style joint Q-K projection (open, not killed; this idea differs by using CCA rather than training-time MLA). Closest published method: MLA (DeepSeek-V2) proposes joint Q-K during training; AJDGF applies the same invariant post-training. The structural difference: AJDGF derives from the gauge freedom of the (W_Q, W_K) attention-score computation, not from distillation. CCA applied to (W_Q, W_K) pairs post-training has not appeared in the compression literature to this author's knowledge. The mathematical identity (attention scores depend only on M = W_Q W_K^T, not on the individual factors) is elementary but has not been used as a no-retraining compression entry point.

**Smallest experiment.** Claim: CCA-based joint (W_Q, W_K) compression at rank r=64 per head achieves ΔNLL < +0.5 nats on Qwen3-1.7B-Base, better than independent SVD truncation at equal bit budget. Procedure: (1) For each layer-head pair, compute CCA of W_Q and W_K using scipy.linalg.svd on the product; truncate at r=64; replace W_Q and W_K in the model. (2) Evaluate ΔNLL on WikiText-2. Runtime: CCA per head is O(d d_k^2) ≈ O(2048 × 128^2) = 34M ops × 448 heads = ~15 min total on Ryzen. GO threshold: ΔNLL < +0.50 nats. NO-GO: ΔNLL > +1.5 nats (worse than or equal to independent truncation); establishes that the gauge freedom does not help because the matrices are already approximately aligned.

**Primary risk.** scipy.linalg.svd CCA assumes full-rank matrices; if W_Q or W_K are degenerate in a way that causes CCA instability (ill-conditioned cross-covariance), the factorization can produce large numerical errors. Mitigation: regularize the cross-covariance with ridge λ=1e-3; check condition number before proceeding.

## F3-A — Residual-Stream Covariance Laplacian Eigendecomposition (RSCLE)

**Track A** — arch-transposition: reformulate each layer's residual-stream transformation in the eigenbasis of the empirically measured covariance operator, where attention + FFN together are a perturbation of the identity (by CF2, cos(h_L, h_{L+1}) ≈ 0.99).

CF2 establishes that adjacent residual states are nearly parallel (cos ≈ 0.99). This means the per-token incremental update δ_L = h_{L+1} - h_L lies almost entirely in a low-dimensional subspace orthogonal to h_L. Let C^(L) = E[δ_L δ_L^T] be the empirical covariance of the update vectors (measurable from calibration). C^(L) is a low-rank PSD matrix (the updates are small and directionally concentrated). The eigenspectrum of C^(L) defines the "active" directions — the directions in d=2048-dimensional residual space where each layer actually writes. If the top r eigenvectors of C^(L) capture fraction f of the update variance, then: (1) the (d-r)-dimensional null space of C^(L) is effectively the identity map at layer L, and (2) all layer-L operations (attention + FFN) only need to be evaluated in the r-dimensional active subspace. This is a NO-SGD equivalence: the computation graph at inference can be reformulated as "project x into r active directions, apply attention+FFN in r-dim, project back" — with NO change to the trained weights, only a change of basis. The Laplacian framing: C^(L) = I - cos(h_L, h_{L+1}) ≈ "spectral graph Laplacian" of the transition graph on residual space; the active subspace is the low-frequency component.

This differs from truncating weights (which CF8 kills) — the weights are unchanged; the computation is reorganized into the subspace basis. The active-subspace routing replaces the full-dimensional matmul with a cheaper r-dimensional one, at the cost of two projection steps of size r × d.

**Residency arithmetic.** If r = 256 (12.5% of d=2048) captures fraction f=0.90 of update variance at each layer: (1) Attention GEMV: projecting q into 256-dim costs 2×256×2048=1M FMAs; the attention within 256-dim costs 16×256×T (T=context length). Original cost: 4×2048^2=16.7M FMAs per layer. Reduction: ~4× in attention GEMV compute if active r=256 suffices. (2) FFN GEMV: no residency reduction (weights are unchanged, stored as usual), but the matmul bandwidth is dominated by weight loading, not the activations — so compute savings don't translate directly to tok/s. The mechanism primarily reduces FMA compute, not weight residency. At 7.28 GiB the target is tok/s not bytes, so FMA reduction is the payoff: on Qwen3-1.7B at DRAM bandwidth-limited inference (~11.5 GB/s), each token requires 2×3.2 GB = 6.4 GB of weight reads → ~0.56 sec/token. If active-subspace routing reduces the effective attention GEMV work by 4× for a 28-layer model, the compute overhead (2×r×d per layer) is 2×256×2048×28=29M FMAs — negligible vs the 28×4×2048^2=932M FMAs baseline. The tok/s gain is limited by weight bandwidth not compute, so direct tok/s uplift is modest; however this idea pairs with quantization as a co-design (lower-rank activations → INT4 quantization error reduced in the active subspace).

**Novelty gloss.** No kill-list item directly matches. Closest published method: Active Subspace Methods in numerical UQ (Constantine 2015) — but applied to neural network activations not weights, and without the residual-stream covariance framing. Spectral graph theory meets transformer residual-stream update structure: the residual stream update covariance has not been directly characterized as an object for subspace routing. Closest in spirit: "Intrinsic Dimensionality" framing (CF15* in the in-flight Opus pipeline, unverified in v2) — RSCLE derives the active subspace from C^(L) directly without relying on CF15's unconfirmed numbers.

**Smallest experiment.** Claim: For Qwen3-1.7B-Base, the top-r eigenvectors of the per-layer residual update covariance C^(L) = E[δ_L δ_L^T] capture >0.80 of update variance at r ≤ 256 in at least 20/28 layers. Procedure: (1) Run 200-token calibration pass, record h_L and h_{L+1} at each layer; compute δ_L = h_{L+1} - h_L. (2) Compute covariance C^(L) = (1/N) Σ_i δ_L^i δ_L^{i,T} ∈ R^{2048×2048}. (3) Eigendecompose; measure cumulative variance. Runtime: 28 layers × 2048×2048 covariance = ~15 min on Ryzen (dominated by outer-product accumulation). GO threshold: var@256 > 0.80 in ≥20 layers. NO-GO finding: update variance is spread across all d=2048 directions (no concentrated active subspace exists), which would mean residual updates are isotropic — this would be a new structural finding (contradicting intuition from near-constant cosine similarity) and would itself be a confirmable CF.

**Primary risk.** C^(L) is computed from a small calibration set; the eigenvectors may overfit to calibration distribution. Mitigation: use train/test split on calibration; report held-out variance fraction.

## F4-B — Outlier-Channel Static Spine Quantization (OCSSQ)

**Track B** — compression: a three-tier quantization scheme derived algebraically from CF3's K-dependent Jaccard structure.

CF3 is a measured structural fact: at K=0.1% (top ~2 channels by absolute magnitude), Jaccard = 0.718 (nearly static); at K=1% (top ~20 channels), Jaccard = 0.308 (token-dynamic); at K=5-10%, Jaccard ≈ 0.25 (token-dynamic). This K-dependent transition is not an engineering observation — it is a fixed-point condition: the top-0.1% channels are a *static attractor* of the outlier distribution, and the 0.1%-1% band is a *fluctuating shell* around that attractor. The first-principles derivation: let x ∈ R^d be a residual stream vector. Decompose x = x_spine + x_shell + x_bulk where x_spine has support on the top-2 channels (always the same 2), x_shell has support on channels 3-20 (per-token varying), x_bulk is the remainder. By CF3's Jaccard measurements, x_spine has a near-deterministic channel identity (decomposes to 2 FP16 values per token with static channel indices — essentially free). x_shell requires per-token dynamic INT8 with dynamic channel routing (18 channels). x_bulk (2028 channels) is quantized INT4 statically. This three-tier decomposition is algebraically derived: the tier boundaries are set by the Jaccard phase transitions in CF3, not by hyperparameter search.

**Residency arithmetic.** Activation quantization does not reduce weight residency; it reduces the *activation bandwidth* during attention score computation. For d=2048: x_spine = 2 FP16 = 4 bytes; x_shell = 18 INT8 = 18 bytes; x_bulk = 2028 × 0.5 bytes (INT4) = 1014 bytes. Total: 4+18+1014 = 1036 bytes vs 2048×2 = 4096 bytes (BF16 baseline). Activation compression: ~4×. The activation bandwidth cost during attention Q/K computation (x projected into W_Q, W_K) is proportional to activation volume. A 4× activation size reduction → attention GEMV input bandwidth × 0.25. On DRAM-bandwidth-limited Qwen3-1.7B: per-token activation volume is ~d × n_layers × 2 bytes = 2048 × 28 × 2 bytes = 114 KB; weight volume is ~6.4 GB per token. Activations are << weights in bandwidth, so activation compression saves <2% total bandwidth. The payoff is primarily in KV cache residency: at d_head=128, 16 heads, K/V stored per token: 2×16×128×2 = 8 KB per token per layer, 28 layers = 224 KB/token. At INT4 activation quantization applied to K/V: 4× reduction → 56 KB/token. At 4K context: 4K × (224-56) KB = 672 MB saved. This is meaningful for long-context.

**Novelty gloss.** Closest kill-list item: RAOK (R2-Aware Outlier-Keyed Activation Codebook, Track B R6 deferred, not killed). OCSSQ differs: RAOK is a per-channel codebook approach; OCSSQ derives tier *boundaries* from CF3 Jaccard phase transitions (0.1%/1%/remainder) and applies arithmetic-tier quantization — no codebook lookup, just channel-index masking. Closest published method: LLM.int8() (threshold-based outlier handling) and SmoothQuant (smooth outliers into weights). OCSSQ uses the Jaccard transition structure to derive thresholds from calibration rather than using a global norm threshold; the 0.1% / 1% boundary is a CF3-specific number unique to Qwen3-family characterization.

**Smallest experiment.** Claim: The CF3 Jaccard phase transition at K=0.1%/1% defines stable tier boundaries such that a three-tier activation quantization (FP16 spine / INT8 shell / INT4 bulk) achieves ΔNLL < +0.30 nats on Qwen3-1.7B-Base. Procedure: (1) From calibration, identify the 2 static channels (K=0.1%) and 18 token-dynamic channels (K=1% minus K=0.1%) per layer. (2) Apply three-tier quantization to residual stream activations at every layer. (3) Evaluate on WikiText-2 held-out. Runtime: calibration pass (~10 min), inference with quantized activations (~20 min). GO: ΔNLL < +0.30 nats AND KV cache 4× reduction demonstrated. NO-GO: ΔNLL > +1.0 nats (tier boundaries from CF3 Jaccard don't translate cleanly to quantization-optimal partitions); finding is that Jaccard and quantization-sensitivity are not aligned measures.

**Primary risk.** CF3 characterizes activation *channel identity* by Jaccard, but ΔNLL under quantization depends on activation *magnitude sensitivity* — a different measure. The channels with the highest Jaccard stability may not be the channels most sensitive to quantization error. Mitigation: cross-validate tier assignment by comparing Jaccard-derived tiers vs Hessian-sensitivity-derived tiers (5 minutes of calibration).

## F5-A — Spectral Gauge Freedom in W_V/W_O Product (SGFVO)

**Track A** — arch-transposition: exploit the gauge symmetry of the W_V/W_O matrix product to reduce the effective computation dimension.

In standard multi-head attention, the output of head ℓ at layer i is: out_ℓ = A_ℓ x W_V^(i,ℓ) W_O^(i,ℓ) where A_ℓ is the attention score matrix. W_V^(i,ℓ) ∈ R^{d × d_head} and W_O^(i,ℓ) ∈ R^{d_head × d}. Define the head-output operator P^(i,ℓ) = W_V^(i,ℓ) W_O^(i,ℓ) ∈ R^{d × d}. The attention mechanism depends on P^(i,ℓ), not on W_V and W_O individually — the same gauge freedom as the (W_Q, W_K) pair: any invertible T yields equivalent P via W_V T, T^{-1} W_O. **The rank of P^(i,ℓ) is at most d_head = 128** (since P = V-factor × O-factor is a rank-d_head matrix). This is not an approximation — it is an identity. P^(i,ℓ) has exact rank ≤ 128 regardless of training. The full (d × d = 2048 × 2048) W_V W_O product can always be replaced by its rank-128 factored form. The W_V/W_O spectra have NOT been measured on Qwen3-1.7B (CF11 only measured W_Q and W_K). The first-principles prediction: if P = W_V W_O is rank-128 exactly, we can replace "compute W_V·x then W_O" with a single 2048×128 matrix (P's SVD left factor times singular values) applied to the attention-weighted x. The computation graph change: instead of (x → W_V → d_head-dim → W_O → d-dim), we have (x → P_left → d*-dim → P_right → d-dim) where d* ≤ 128 by construction. This is NOT a rank-reduction approximation — it is the identity for d* = d_head.

The first-principles question layered on top: is the *effective* rank of P^(i,ℓ) substantially below d_head=128? CF11 shows W_Q r_99/d = 0.63 (r_99 ≈ 1293 out of 2048); if W_V has a similar spectrum, then P = W_V W_O might have effective rank << 128. If r_99(P^(i,ℓ)) ≈ 64, then a single 2048×64 projection replaces the W_V + W_O pair entirely — a 4× bandwidth reduction for those weights.

**Residency arithmetic.** Current: W_V: 16×128×2048 = 4M params; W_O: 16×2048×128 = 4M params; total 8M params = 16 MB BF16 per layer × 28 = 448 MB. Exact-rank identity: P per head ∈ R^{2048×2048} at rank ≤ 128 = 2×(2048×128) per head = 524K params per head = 8.4M params all heads = 16.7 MB per layer = 467 MB total. No reduction at exact rank — same as storing W_V and W_O separately. BUT if effective rank r* = 64: P factors at 2×(2048×64) = 524K for all 16 heads (same shape, half the rank) = 233 MB total. Saving: 215 MB vs 448 MB = 48% of (W_V, W_O) residency. For 70B: 16× scale = ~3.4 GB saved in (W_V, W_O) alone at r*=64.

**Novelty gloss.** No kill-list item targets the W_V/W_O product rank. Closest published method: "MHA-to-MLA" post-training conversion (A3, arXiv:2505.12942) shares some geometric intuition but is primarily a Q-K mechanism. The W_V W_O gauge freedom as a no-cost compression primitive has not appeared in any kill-list or survey entry. The exact-rank identity (P has rank ≤ d_head by construction) is elementary, yet no published post-training compression method has used it as the *primary* compression entry point.

**Smallest experiment.** Claim: The W_V W_O product P^(i,ℓ) for Qwen3-1.7B-Base has effective rank r_99 ≤ 96 (i.e., top 96 singular values capture ≥ 99% of the Frobenius norm) in at least 22/28 × 16 heads. Procedure: (1) For each head and layer, compute P = W_V @ W_O ∈ R^{2048×2048} (one matrix multiply per head); (2) Compute SVD; (3) Measure r_99. Runtime: 448 head-matrix-multiplies (2048×128 × 128×2048 each → 67M FMAs) + 448 SVDs (each 2048×2048 = ~minutes; use truncated SVD at r=128 since theoretical max is 128). Total: ~45 min. GO threshold: median r_99 ≤ 96 across heads. NO-GO: median r_99 = 128 (product is full head rank); no compression gain over storing W_V, W_O separately. NO-GO finding: establishes that W_V and W_O are *complementarily* full-rank (neither contains the null space of the other), which itself closes a question about attention weight redundancy.

**Primary risk.** The W_V W_O exact-rank identity is already known; the open question is whether r_99 is substantially below d_head. If W_V and W_O are both nearly full-rank and their product is close to rank-128, there is no gain beyond what separate W_V/W_O storage already provides. Mitigation: run the r_99 measurement first; the mechanism only activates if r_99 << 128.

## F6-B — AERO-Compatible SwiGLU Identity Test at Layer 1 (ASIT) [FREE SWING]

**Track B** — compression with arch-transposition flavor.

CF6 establishes a genuine anomaly: layer 1 of trained Qwen3-1.7B has 36.1% of gate neurons with variance below the foldable threshold (std < 0.05), vs ≤2.5% in all other layers. AERO (arXiv:2410.13060) showed that if silu(W_gate·x) ≈ constant α across all calibration inputs, then W_gate drops out and W_up is replaced by α·W_up — a no-retraining algebraic fold. CF6 confirms this regime holds locally at layer 1 for 36% of neurons. The Free Swing claim: this is not a "tiny free win" but a **diagnostic target**. The question is whether layer 1's gate-variance anomaly traces to an identifiable structure in W_gate^(1) — perhaps a near-null subspace in W_gate^(1) that, when confirmed, predicts which OTHER layers will develop gate near-constancy at longer calibration or slightly different tokenization. If W_gate^(1) has a measurable low-eigenvalue cluster (i.e., a subset of the input-space directions that produce near-zero gate activations regardless of token), this cluster is a STRUCTURAL property of W_gate^(1), not a calibration artifact. Identifying it would enable: (1) immediate 36% layer-1 fold, and (2) a predictive criterion for fold-eligibility in any new model without running full calibration.

The first-principles derivation: gate near-constancy for neuron j means silu(W_gate[j,:]·x) ≈ α_j for all x in the calibration distribution. This requires W_gate[j,:] to lie near the null space of the input covariance C_x = E[x x^T], i.e., the row direction W_gate[j,:] must have small projection onto the high-variance directions of the input. Check: is W_gate^(1)[j,:] ∈ null(C_x^(1)) for the foldable-j neurons? If yes, gate near-constancy is a structural geometric fact (the gate row doesn't "see" the input distribution), and it propagates to any model trained on a similar data distribution.

**Residency arithmetic.** Immediate win: 36% × 6144 neurons (layer-1 intermediate dim) × (W_gate row 2048 dims) × BF16 = 0.36 × 6144 × 2048 × 2 bytes = 9 MB saved at layer 1. On a 3.2 GB total model this is ~0.3% — genuinely tiny. The structural value is the fold-eligibility predictor: if the criterion is geometric (W_gate[j,:] near null(C_x)), it applies across model families without re-running calibration. On Qwen3-70B (estimated 50× weight scale), if 36% anomaly propagates to even 3 layers: 3 × 0.36 × (70B's intermediate ~ 28672) × 2048 × 2 bytes ≈ 750 MB freed — material.

**Novelty gloss.** Closest kill-list item: SDZC (SwiGLU Dead-Zone Gate Collapse) — killed as a global compression scheme, but explicitly noted "Layer 1 anomaly is interesting but isolated." ASIT is not proposing global compression; it proposes identifying the *geometric origin* of the anomaly and deriving a structural criterion. Closest published method: AERO (arXiv:2410.13060) — AERO requires running full calibration; ASIT's proposed criterion (W_gate row projected onto null(C_x)) would enable zero-calibration fold-eligibility prediction. The structural move (null-space alignment as fold criterion) is not in AERO or in any kill-list entry.

**Smallest experiment.** Claim: For foldable neurons at layer 1 (CF6 std < 0.05 criterion), ‖W_gate^(1)[j,:] Π_{C_x^(1)}^{null}‖ / ‖W_gate^(1)[j,:]‖ > 0.70, where Π_{C_x^(1)}^{null} is the projection onto the bottom-eigenvalue subspace of C_x^(1). Procedure: (1) Collect ~200 tokens of layer-1 input activations; compute C_x^(1); find bottom-r eigenvectors (r=512 or less). (2) Project W_gate^(1) rows onto this subspace; measure per-neuron projection fraction. (3) Compare foldable vs non-foldable neurons. Runtime: 30 minutes. GO threshold: mean projection fraction for foldable neurons > 0.70 AND significantly higher than for non-foldable neurons (p < 0.01). NO-GO: projection fractions are indistinguishable between foldable and non-foldable; the anomaly is a calibration artifact, not structural.

**Primary risk.** The foldability criterion (std < 0.05) may be calibration-sensitive — different calibration data may flip which neurons are "foldable." Mitigation: test with two non-overlapping calibration sets; if fold-neuron sets are stable (Jaccard > 0.70), the structural interpretation holds.

## F7-A — Cross-Layer W_Q Shared-Basis MLA Reconstruction (CQSMR)

**Track A** — arch-transposition: if CQSGC's cross-layer shared subspace exists (GO on F1-A), replace all 28 layers' W_Q with a single shared low-rank basis plus cheap per-layer projection coefficients — the MLA equivalent derived from first principles rather than training.

This idea is explicitly conditional on F1-A GO. State it as a cascade second rung. If var@D*=128 > 0.80 in F1-A: let U ∈ R^{2048×128} be the shared right singular space from the stacked-SVD. For each layer ℓ, W_Q^(ℓ) ≈ C^(ℓ) U^T where C^(ℓ) ∈ R^{2048×128} = W_Q^(ℓ) U. Inference changes: instead of x → W_Q^(ℓ) → d_Q=2048-dim per layer, we have (1) x → U^T (d×128 projection, *shared across all layers*, computed once per token at the input) = z^128 ∈ R^{128}; (2) per-layer: z^128 → C^(ℓ,^T) → d_Q. This is algebraically equivalent to the original W_Q^(ℓ) x if var@128 = 1.0; it is a first-order approximation if var@128 = 0.80. Storage: U is 2048×128 = 0.5 MB (shared, loaded once); C^(ℓ) for all ℓ is 128×2048×28 = 14.7 MB. Total: 15.2 MB vs original 224 MB W_Q. This is a 14.7× reduction in W_Q residency with a single-token projection overhead.

**Residency arithmetic.** (Conditional on F1-A GO.) W_Q residency: 15.2 MB vs 224 MB. Computation change per token: 28 × 2048×128 GEMV replaced by 1 × 2048×128 (shared U^T projection) + 28 × 128×2048 (per-layer cheap projection). Net GEMV count: identical. But weight bytes read per token: 14.7 MB (all C^(ℓ)) + 0.5 MB (U, cached in L3) vs 224 MB (all W_Q). On DRAM-bandwidth-limited inference: saving 209 MB of W_Q reads per token. At 11.5 GB/s DRAM bandwidth: 209 MB / 11.5 = 18 ms per token saved. If baseline tok/s is ~1 tok/s for 3.2 GB Qwen3-1.7B at 11.5 GB/s: 18 ms / ~278 ms expected token time = 6.5% speed gain per token from W_Q alone.

**Novelty gloss.** Closest kill-list item: none (F1-A is not on kill list; CQSMR is its second rung, conditional). Closest published method: MLA post-training (A3, arXiv:2505.12942) shares the factored-W_Q concept; CQSMR differs by using a single cross-layer U — a stronger claim that has no published post-training counterpart. **Smallest experiment.** Conditional on F1-A GO (var@128 > 0.80). Claim: replacing all 28 W_Q^(ℓ) with C^(ℓ) U^T factorization (U from stacked SVD, C^(ℓ) = W_Q^(ℓ) U) achieves ΔNLL < +1.5 nats on Qwen3-1.7B-Base. Procedure: build U from stacked SVD (already computed in F1-A); reconstruct W_Q^(ℓ) = C^(ℓ) U^T; swap into model; evaluate ΔNLL. Runtime: 10 minutes (reconstruction + eval). GO: ΔNLL < +1.5 nats. NO-GO: ΔNLL > +3 nats; shared basis incurs too much per-layer reconstruction error.

**Primary risk.** The reconstruction error from var@128 ≈ 0.80 (20% variance not captured) may accumulate across 28 layers, producing catastrophic ΔNLL even if each layer's error is small.
