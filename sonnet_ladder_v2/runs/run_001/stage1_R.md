## MCQKV — Multi-Cascade Attention Compression (MCQKV) — R / Track B

**Mechanism.** Track B. CF11 establishes W_Q r_99/d ≈ 0.63 and a 16:1 head-redundancy pattern (16 query heads span a ~128-dim joint subspace); CF11 also reports W_K at K=512 gives ΔNLL=+0.29 nats. AQFKV found W_K r_99=814 (vs d=2048; r_99/d ≈ 0.40). These two findings compose multiplicatively on the attention weight residency. Qwen3-72B has 64 attention layers, each with W_Q (d×d), W_K (d×kv_d), W_V (d×kv_d), W_O (d×d) at d=8192. Replacing W_Q with a rank-K global-head-shared factorization (U_Q: d×K, V_Q: K×d) and W_K with rank-K SVD (U_K: d×K, V_K: K×kv_d) across all layers reduces the four attention weight matrices. CF11's global-K=128 finding was obtained on 1.7B-scale (d=2048); the 72B scale has d=8192 and GQA (W_K is shared across heads). The cascade is: (rung 1) measure W_Q and W_K spectra on Qwen3-1.7B across all 28 layers to establish per-layer K_99 distributions; (rung 2) project to 72B residency arithmetic using CF11's cross-scale spectrum homogeneity assumption; (rung 3) deploy at the 7.28 GiB Ryzen tier. W_V and W_O spectra are untested (SUMMARY.md explicitly names them as open); their GO status opens a second compression axis with no current kill evidence.

**Residency arithmetic.** Qwen3-72B bf16 ≈ 144 GB. At IQ4_XS (0.55 bpw), attention + MLP weights total ~5.35 GiB for a 7B; for 72B the same ratio gives ~39 GiB. Attention weights are ≈4 × d² × L × bpw. At d=8192, L=64, GQA kv_heads=8 (kv_d = 8 × 128 = 1024): W_Q = 64×8192×8192 = 4.3B params, W_K = 64×8192×1024 = 0.54B, W_V = 64×8192×1024 = 0.54B, W_O = 64×8192×8192 = 4.3B. At bf16 that is ~(4.3+0.54+0.54+4.3)×2 ≈ 19.5 GB for attention, ~77 GB for MLP (28B MLP params at bf16). CF11 established W_Q: K=128/2048 = 6% of rows suffices (8× compression on W_Q). At 72B with d=8192, the analogous global-head K scales as d/16 = 512 — one head's worth of capacity for 64 heads. W_Q compression at K=512/8192 = 6%: from 4.3B to (8192×512 + 512×8192)×64 / 10⁹ ≈ 0.54B params, saving 3.76B. W_K at K=512/8192: same saving. W_V and W_O are assumed GO pending measurement. Conservative scenario (W_Q + W_K only, both at K=512): saves ~(3.76+0.27)×2 GB ≈ 8 GB in bf16. In INT4 that's ~4 GB. Optimistic (W_V and W_O also at K=512): saves ~16 GB bf16 ≈ 8 GB INT4. Starting from 39 GiB at IQ4_XS: target 31–35 GiB with W_Q+W_K compression. Quality cost: CF11 reports +0.29 nats at W_K K=512 (2×); W_Q K=128 (8×) is +0.98 nats on 1.7B; at K=512 (2× on 1.7B) W_Q is +0.03 nats. Analogously: at 72B, K=512 for W_Q should be mild. Estimated total ΔNLL ≤ 0.5 nats combined (both matrices at K=512). Tok/s at 35 GiB on NVMe (CF13*-independent path: NVMe ~3 GB/s theoretical; at 35 GiB model, 35/3 ≈ 11.7 s/token → 0.085 tok/s, too slow. But DRAM-resident scenario: 7.28 GiB budget at INT2 or sub-1-bit for MLP, with attention weights compressed to ~10 GiB INT4 total. For DRAM-resident 7B inference at 11.5 GB/s DRAM BW with 7 GiB model: 7 GiB / 11.5 GB/s ≈ 0.6 s/token → 1.7 tok/s. Compression helps proportionally: from 7.28 GiB to ~6 GiB with attention saving = 7.28/6 × 1.7 ≈ 2.1 tok/s on a 7B-equivalent model. For 70B DRAM-resident we need sub-1-bpw for MLP weights too — attention compression alone doesn't close the gap; it is one rung of a larger cascade.

**Novelty gloss.** Closest kill-list item: R3-A AQFKV established W_Q global rank compression; MCQKV extends it to the full attention weight class (W_Q + W_K + W_V + W_O jointly) and targets 72B residency, which AQFKV did not. Closest published method: MLA (DeepSeek-V2) and A3 (arXiv:2505.12942) which uses attention-logit-error minimization post-training; MCQKV is weight-Frobenius global-head-shared SVD applied post-hoc without retraining, empirically motivated by our CF11 measurement (not by training-time design). The structural difference is that CF11 gives us the actual spectrum on Qwen3 family, not a theoretical prediction — the compression ratio is derived from measured singular value decay, not from an architectural prior.

**Smallest experiment.** Testable claim: W_V and W_O on Qwen3-1.7B have r_99/d ≤ 0.8 (analogous to W_K at 0.79). Measure SVD spectrum of all four attention weight matrices (W_Q, W_K, W_V, W_O) on all 28 layers of Qwen3-1.7B-Base bf16. Runtime: ~15 min per matrix × 4 matrices × 28 layers ≈ 4 hours total on Ryzen. Go: r_99/d ≤ 0.8 for W_V and W_O (both). No-go: r_99/d ≥ 0.9 for W_V or W_O — compresses the cascade to W_Q + W_K only (~8 GB saving at 72B vs 16 GB optimistic). Even on no-go, the experiment completes the Qwen3 weight-class characterization (W_gate ≈ 1.0, W_up ≈ 1.0, W_Q ≈ 0.63, W_K ≈ 0.79, W_V = ?, W_O = ?), which is a structural finding the pipeline does not yet have. Numerical threshold: GO if mean(r_99_W_V / d, r_99_W_O / d) ≤ 0.80 across layers 5–27.

**Primary risk.** W_V and W_O may be full-rank like W_gate/W_up (the MLP-side pattern), in which case the cascade is limited to W_Q + W_K compression and the 8 GB saving (not 16 GB), keeping 70B DRAM-resident out of reach. Mitigation: run the spectrum measurement first (rung 1); if W_V/W_O are full-rank, pivot to composing MCQKV with a separate MLP quantization path (RAOK or PDAP) to close the residency gap.

## CROSS-Q — Cross-Layer Query Subspace Sharing (CROSS-Q) — R / Track A

**Mechanism.** Track A (computation graph changes: W_Q matrices are replaced by a shared projection factory + per-layer residual). CF11 established that 16 query heads across a single layer collapse to a ~128-dim joint subspace. The Reach-level question is: do 28 (or 64) W_Q matrices across all layers also share a dominant joint subspace? If yes, a single cross-layer Q-basis B ∈ R^{d × K_global} (K_global << d × L) can represent all layer-wise W_Q as W_Q^ℓ ≈ B · C_ℓ where C_ℓ ∈ R^{K_global × d} is a per-layer thin coefficient matrix. This is architecturally different from per-layer SVD (CF11): it would expose cross-layer structural redundancy that per-layer compression misses entirely. The primitive is PCA-stacking: stack all L W_Q matrices as rows into a matrix M ∈ R^{L·d × d}, compute its top-K singular vectors — that's the shared basis B. The operation requires no calibration data, no retraining. The structural motivation: residual-stream continuity (CF2: cos ≈ 0.99 between adjacent h_L) means query representations evolve slowly layer-to-layer, biasing W_Q to track a slowly-rotating subspace. Not CF8 MLP rank reduction (W_Q explicitly carved out by CF11). Cross-layer sharing reduces the storage of W_Q from L × d² to (d × K_global + L × K_global × d), a saving of L/(1 + L·K_global/d) ≈ (for K_global = 256, L = 64, d = 8192): 64 / (1 + 64×256/8192) = 64 / 3 ≈ 21×. The query projection at inference becomes: q = (B · C_ℓ) · h, which can be evaluated as q = B · (C_ℓ · h) — two smaller matmuls instead of one large one. This is a Track A change: the forward pass computation graph changes shape.

**Residency arithmetic.** Qwen3-72B: L=64 layers, d=8192. W_Q per layer (assuming full square, ignoring GQA for Q): 8192×8192 = 67M params. All 64 W_Q: 4.3B params. At bf16: 8.6 GB. With cross-layer shared basis B at K_global=256: B = 8192×256 = 2.1M params; per-layer C_ℓ = 256×8192 = 2.1M params × 64 layers = 134M params. Total: 136M params ≈ 0.27 GB bf16. Reduction: 8.6 GB → 0.27 GB = 32× on W_Q alone. In practice K_global > 256 may be needed (to be measured); at K_global=1024: total = 8192×1024 + 1024×8192×64 = 8.4M + 536M = 544M params = 1.09 GB. Still 8× savings on W_Q. Combined with IQ4_XS for MLP: 39 GiB baseline - 8.6 GB W_Q + 1.09 GB replacement = 32 GiB. At 11.5 GB/s DRAM BW: 32 GiB / 11.5 = 2.8 s/token → 0.36 tok/s. Still NVMe-bound at 70B. But: compose with attention-side KV quantization and MLP codebook quantization. This rung removes 7–8 GB from the 39 GiB baseline — enough to shift the system from "never fits in 7.28 GiB" to "fits on NVMe with 20% better throughput." The cascade ceiling is not DRAM-resident 70B from this rung alone; it is one rung of a 3-rung stack. Quality cost: if K_global=1024 captures 95%+ of joint W_Q variance, ΔNLL ≤ 0.2 nats (conservative extrapolation from CF11 per-layer findings). Verify CF13*/CF14* (residual stream correlation structure predicting cross-layer W_Q alignment) ONLY as motivation; the experiment does not rely on those numbers.

**Novelty gloss.** Closest kill-list item: CLUBS/RSTD (cross-layer Tucker on W_up) — KILLED because W_up is full-rank. CROSS-Q targets W_Q, which CF11 confirmed is NOT full-rank (r_99/d = 0.63), placing this in a class explicitly carved out of the full-rank kill. Closest published method: Parameter Sharing in Transformers (various) and Cross-Layer Attention Sharing — both require training-time design. CROSS-Q is a post-hoc PCA-stack operation on the trained weights of an existing model, enabled by the empirical discovery that W_Q has concentrated spectrum (our CF11 measurement, not a design choice). The structural difference from published weight-sharing is that no retraining occurs and the basis is derived from the measured spectral structure, not imposed at training time.

**Smallest experiment.** Testable claim: the top-K singular vectors of the stacked W_Q matrix (all 28 layers of Qwen3-1.7B, stacked as a 28×2048 × 2048 matrix reshaped to 57344 × 2048) account for ≥ 70% of the total W_Q variance at K=256. Runtime: SVD of 57344×2048 matrix — can be done iteratively via randomized SVD (sklearn/torch) in <30 min on Ryzen. Go: top-256 singular vectors explain ≥ 70% of joint W_Q variance AND per-layer reconstruction ΔNLL < 1.0 nat at K=256 coefficients. No-go: <50% explained variance — the cross-layer basis has no structural coherence, which is itself a finding (W_Q subspaces rotate independently layer-to-layer, inconsistent with residual-stream continuity implication). Threshold: measure variance explained at K ∈ {64, 128, 256, 512}; plot elbow; GO if 70% at K ≤ d/8 = 256.

**Primary risk.** W_Q subspaces may rotate freely layer-to-layer (CF2 shows h_L residuals are near-parallel, but W_Q maps from residual to query space — the rotation could be uncorrelated with residual continuity). Mitigation: the smallest experiment settles this in <1 day; if NO-GO, the structural finding (cross-layer W_Q incoherence) is valuable for closing out the attention-compression search space.

## RAOK-70B — R2-Grounded Outlier-Keyed Activation Cascade to 70B (RAOK-70B) — R / Track B

**Mechanism.** Track B. RAOK (R2-Aware Outlier-Keyed Activation Codebook) was deferred at Track B R6 Stage 4 with the note "DEFER — most CF9-robust idea, no imported theory; runs after lm_head spectrum question is resolved." The lm_head question is now resolved (CF12: tied lm_head is full-rank; untied path is open but not blocking RAOK). RAOK directly operationalizes CF3 (K-dependent outlier dynamicity): pin top-0.1% channels (K≈2 channels at d=2048) statically as FP16 (Jaccard 0.718 at K=0.1%); treat K=1% channels (≈20 channels) with per-token INT8 dynamic pinning (Jaccard 0.31); quantize remaining 99.9% to INT4 statically. The Reach extension of bare RAOK is: (a) verify the three-tier scheme on Qwen3-1.7B (the deferred RAOK experiment itself), (b) measure the effective bpw achieved, (c) cascade with CF11-derived W_Q compression (MCQKV rung) to build the most CF-grounded 70B deployment path available. The three-tier codebook on activations during GEMV changes the byte-stream the matmul consumes: 2 channels at FP16 (32 bits), ~20 channels at INT8 (8 bits), ~2026 channels at INT4 (4 bits). Effective activation bpw: (2×16 + 20×8 + 2026×4) / 2048 ≈ (32 + 160 + 8104) / 2048 ≈ 4.05 bpw on activations — nearly INT4 but with exact handling of the static and dynamic outlier channels. The key CF9-compliance point: the three-tier structure is derived from the measured Jaccard at K=0.1% and K=1% (CF3), not from any imported theorem. The mechanism is a calibration-free structural derivation from measured outlier statistics.

**Residency arithmetic.** RAOK addresses activation quantization, not weight quantization — it reduces activation bandwidth during GEMV, not model file size. The relevant bottleneck is GEMV compute + memory bandwidth during inference. For DRAM-resident 7B at INT4 weights: activation bpw ≈ INT4 = 4 bpw. With RAOK three-tier, the GEMV engine can use wider AVX2 INT4 paths for 99% of channels, reserving FP16 only for 2 channels (0.1%). On DRAM-bandwidth-bound inference, the activation path is secondary (weight bandwidth dominates for small batch). For NVMe-offload inference, RAOK reduces the activation volume passed between NVMe-loaded weight chunks, potentially enabling larger batch size. The primary residency lever from RAOK for 70B: compose with W_Q compression (CROSS-Q / MCQKV) and a low-bpw MLP scheme (PDAP, IQ4_XS for most weights). RAOK's contribution in this cascade: reduces GEMV overhead by ~1.5–2.5× (from the R6 Stage 4 estimate), which at NVMe-limited inference translates to fewer stall cycles and potentially higher effective tok/s. Quantitative case: 70B at IQ4_XS = 39 GiB on NVMe. At 3 GB/s NVMe sequential read: 39/3 ≈ 13 s/token = 0.077 tok/s. RAOK reduces per-token compute time by 2× → effectively equivalent to 0.15 tok/s if the bottleneck shifts from weight-load to compute. But at 0.077 tok/s the bottleneck IS weight-load, not compute — so RAOK alone doesn't close the gap. Its value is in the composed cascade as the activation-quantization rung, keeping quality tight while MLP quantization pushes bpw down.

**Novelty gloss.** Closest kill-list item: PDAP (Per-Token Dynamic Activation Outlier Pinning, R2 selected). RAOK is structurally distinct: PDAP tested the dynamicity of the outlier SET; RAOK constructs the CODEBOOK that operationalizes the dynamicity result for production inference. PDAP produced CF3; RAOK consumes CF3 as input. Closest published method: LLM.int8() (two-tier: FP16 outliers + INT8 rest) and SmoothQuant (migrates outliers to weights). RAOK differs in being three-tier and in deriving the tier boundaries from the measured Jaccard curve (CF3) rather than from a fixed percentile threshold. The structural novelty is the CF3-grounded threshold selection (0.1% static / 1% dynamic / rest INT4) as opposed to a hand-tuned binary split.

**Smallest experiment.** Testable claim: the RAOK three-tier activation scheme (2 FP16 + 20 INT8 + 2026 INT4 channels) achieves ΔNLL < 0.3 nats vs bf16 baseline on Qwen3-1.7B when applied to W_up GEMV inputs. Runtime: implement three-tier activation routing in Python on top of existing calibration framework; measure NLL on 512-token WikiText-2 held-out. Estimate ≤ 4 hours on Ryzen for the correctness check. Go: ΔNLL < 0.3 nats with the three-tier split derived from CF3 K-boundaries. No-go: ΔNLL ≥ 0.3 nats — implies the per-token dynamic outlier set (K=1%, Jaccard=0.31) is not stable enough for the INT8 bucket, meaning the dynamic bucket must be wider. Even on no-go, the experiment produces the function relating bucket-width to quality degradation for the Qwen3 family, which directly parameterizes the tradespace for any INT4 activation quantization scheme.

**Primary risk.** The INT8 dynamic bucket for K=1% channels (Jaccard=0.31 between consecutive tokens) may accumulate quality cost layer after layer, causing catastrophic NLL at deep layers even if per-layer cost is small. Mitigation: measure per-layer ΔNLL (not just total) to isolate whether the quality cost concentrates in deep layers (which CF3 says have wider outlier spread at K=10%).

## ACWF — Attention-Cascade Weight Fold (ACWF) — R / Track A

**Mechanism.** Track A. AERO (arXiv:2410.13060) established that removing the SiLU activation in SwiGLU, then algebraically folding W_gate into W_up (since silu(x)≈1 in the near-constant regime) produces a structurally compressed FFN. CF6 showed that globally only 1.5% of neurons have gate variance < 0.05 (the foldable condition), so AERO-style folding does NOT apply to MLP weights in trained Qwen3 globally. But CF11 opened a structurally different target: W_Q has measured head-redundancy at 16:1 ratio. The analogous "fold" question for attention is: the multi-head attention projection W_O (output projection, d_total × d) can be written as W_O = [W_O^1 | ... | W_O^H] where each W_O^h ∈ R^{d/H × d}. If multiple heads are redundant (CF11: 16 heads span only ~128 dims jointly), then many W_O^h head-slices project similar content into the residual stream. A calibration-measured fold: identify the set S of heads whose contribution to the residual stream is below a threshold (measured by ‖W_O^h · v^h‖ averaged over calibration tokens), and replace those heads' W_O^h with a shared low-rank projection. This is a Track A change: the output projection computation graph gains a head-selection routing step. The fold does not require per-head attention scores to change — it changes only the output assembly. Combined with W_Q cross-layer sharing (CROSS-Q), this is a two-rung algebraic cascade on the attention computation: (rung 1) compress W_Q via cross-layer basis; (rung 2) collapse redundant heads in W_O by measuring calibration-based head importance. The AERO precedent — algebraic fold enabled by measured near-constancy — is the template; ACWF is its attention-side analog enabled by CF11's head-redundancy measurement.

**Residency arithmetic.** W_O for 72B Qwen3: d×d = 8192×8192 × 64 layers at bf16 = 8.6 GB. If 75% of heads are effectively redundant (CF11 says 16:1 head-to-subspace ratio suggests 15/16 heads carry redundant information), a head-pruned W_O using the top 4 heads' directions plus a shared basis reduces W_O storage by ~75%, saving ~6.5 GB. This is aggressive; a conservative 50% head collapse saves ~4.3 GB. At IQ4_XS baseline of 39 GiB: 39 - 4.3 = 34.7 GiB. Still NVMe-bound for 70B. The residency payoff is real but not sufficient alone for DRAM-resident 70B — it is the attention rung of a larger cascade. The AERO-analog algebraic identity is the load-bearing novelty: unlike MCQKV (which just applies SVD), ACWF uses calibration-measured head importance to derive a merge + zero-out that is algebraically exact for the measured zero-output heads. Quality cost: heads whose calibration-average output ‖W_O^h · v^h‖ < ε contribute < ε to the residual per token — if ε is calibrated at the 5th-percentile norm, ΔNLL should be < 0.1 nats on calibration distribution. Requires hold-out verification per CF10 conditioning check.

**Novelty gloss.** Closest kill-list item: R3-A AQFKV (W_Q global rank compression). ACWF targets W_O (the output projection), not W_Q, and uses a calibration-based head-importance fold rather than SVD rank truncation. This is structurally different: the mechanism is a discrete head-selection + merge (Track A), not a continuous low-rank approximation (Track B). Closest published method: head pruning / attention head importance (Michel et al. 2019, "Are Sixteen Heads Really Better than One?") — but that work prunes heads entirely. ACWF does not prune; it MERGES redundant heads into a shared low-rank projection that preserves their collective output direction. The algebraic identity (redundant heads collapse to a shared projection without information loss on the calibration distribution) is the move that distinguishes ACWF from head pruning.

**Smallest experiment.** Testable claim: ≥ 50% of attention heads in Qwen3-1.7B have calibration-average output norm ‖W_O^h · v^h‖ < 10% of the max-head norm, identifying them as candidates for the fold. Runtime: run calibration forward pass on 200 prompts, collect per-head W_O^h · v^h norms across all layers and heads. ~30 min on Ryzen. Go: ≥ 50% of (layer, head) pairs fall below the 10% norm threshold AND the top-4 heads per layer account for ≥ 75% of total output norm. No-go: head importance is uniform (all heads contribute similarly) — implies CF11's W_Q head-redundancy does NOT extend to W_O head output magnitudes, which is a structural finding distinguishing query-side vs output-side head behavior.

**Primary risk.** Head output importance is token-dependent (some heads activate on specific token types) and the calibration average may not reflect held-out distribution — folding token-class-specific heads risks ΔNLL spikes on specific inputs. Mitigation: measure per-token-type head norms (normal vs BOS/separator per CF3 stratification) before selecting the fold set; only fold heads that are uniformly low-norm across token types.

## ATTN-NVMe-SPLIT — Attention-Resident MLP-NVMe Tiered Pipeline (ATTN-NVMe-SPLIT) — R / Track B

**Mechanism.** Track B. The empirical picture from CF8 + CF11 is asymmetric: MLP weights (W_gate, W_up, W_down) are full-rank and must be quantized (not compressed structurally), while attention weights (W_Q, W_K) are substantially compressible via rank reduction. This asymmetry motivates a two-tier residency strategy: keep ALL attention weights (W_Q, W_K, W_V, W_O) DRAM-resident after cross-layer compression (CROSS-Q + MCQKV), and stream only MLP weights from NVMe. The load-bearing structural argument: attention weights, after compression, fit in DRAM alongside the KV cache and the activation buffer; MLP weights, which resist structural compression, must be streamed from NVMe at IQ4_XS or similar. The split exploits the empirically measured asymmetry (CF8 vs CF11) to assign each weight class to the storage tier that matches its compressibility. This is different from "generic offload" because the tier assignment is derived from the measured spectra — attention goes to DRAM because it's measured-compressible, MLP goes to NVMe because it's measured-incompressible. At 72B: compressed attention weights ≈ 1–2 GiB (using CROSS-Q at K=256 for W_Q, MCQKV for W_K, assume W_V and W_O are similar to W_K); MLP weights at IQ4_XS ≈ 28 GiB (28B MLP params × 0.55 bpw / 8). The DRAM budget of 7.28 GiB holds compressed attention (≈2 GiB) + KV cache (≈0.5 GiB at 4K context) + activation buffers (≈0.5 GiB) = 3 GiB, leaving 4 GiB headroom for the OS + the resident weight buffer for prefetch. MLP weights stream from NVMe at 3 GB/s (theoretical); at 28 GiB total, one forward pass reads ~28/64 = 437 MB of MLP per token (one layer per decode step if fully sequential — but llama.cpp loads all layers for each token). The bottleneck is the full-model NVMe read per token: 28 GiB / 3 GB/s = 9.3 s/token for MLP alone. To reach 3 tok/s, we need NVMe reads to be non-blocking — which requires prefetch. The ATTN-NVMe-SPLIT strategy enables prefetch by keeping the compute thread busy on DRAM-resident attention (which is now faster due to compression) while NVMe DMA prefetches the next-layer MLP chunks. The attention computation time with compressed weights creates a compute window for the prefetch. This is the cascade: (1) measure compressed attention compute time per layer; (2) check if it overlaps with NVMe DMA time for MLP chunk; (3) if yes, the compute-I/O overlap achieves non-blocking throughput.

**Residency arithmetic.** Compressed attention: W_Q at CROSS-Q K=256 ≈ 0.27 GB (computed above); W_K at K=512 ≈ 0.54 GB; W_V and W_O assumed at K=512 each ≈ 0.54 + 4.3 GB → conservatively 5 GB total. More aggressively (W_O compresses like W_V at K=512): 0.27 + 0.54 + 0.54 + 0.54 = 1.89 GB. Call it 2 GiB for compressed attention. MLP at IQ4_XS: 3 matrices (W_gate, W_up, W_down) × d×4d × 64 layers × 0.55 bpw / 8 = 3 × 8192 × 32768 × 64 × 0.55 / 8 bytes = 3 × 8192 × 32768 × 64 × 0.069 bytes ≈ 3 × 17.2 GB = 51.6 GB — too large for NVMe model streaming at 3 tok/s. Scaling back: at 7B not 70B, DRAM-resident path: 7B attention compressed to ≈0.2 GiB, 7B MLP at IQ4_XS ≈ 3 GiB total. Fits in 7.28 GiB with room to spare. For 70B, ATTN-NVMe-SPLIT enables: DRAM holds 2 GiB compressed attention + buffers; NVMe holds 51 GiB MLP. At 3 GB/s NVMe: 51 GiB / 3 = 17 s/token for MLP → NOT 3 tok/s without further MLP compression. Conclusion: ATTN-NVMe-SPLIT is a necessary but not sufficient rung; it must compose with aggressive MLP quantization. Its unique contribution is enabling DRAM-resident attention at compressed size, freeing the NVMe slot entirely for MLP and making the prefetch overlap possible.

**Novelty gloss.** Closest kill-list item: R1/S2 OS-Paging-Aware Weight Layout (killed: "doesn't reduce bpw, just engineers paging"). ATTN-NVMe-SPLIT differs: it does reduce bpw on attention weights (via CROSS-Q/MCQKV compression), and the tier assignment is derived from measured spectra (CF8 asymmetry with CF11), not from paging heuristics. The structural argument is "assign each weight class to the tier that matches its measured compressibility" — a cost-model derivation, not a paging optimization. Closest published method: Apple's LLM-in-a-flash (arXiv:2312.11514) — "Only compute, fetch what you need." ATTN-NVMe-SPLIT's structural difference is that it uses the CF8/CF11 asymmetry to create a permanent DRAM/NVMe split based on compressibility, rather than a dynamic fetch-on-demand scheme.

**Smallest experiment.** Testable claim: on Qwen3-1.7B, compressed attention weights (W_Q at K=256, W_K at K=512) compute in ≤ (MLP NVMe-load time) per layer, enabling overlap. Runtime: on Ryzen, measure attention GEMV time with compressed weights vs NVMe random-read time for a layer's MLP chunk (using a synthetic NVMe read of the same byte count). ≤ 2 hours including setup. Go: attention GEMV time ≥ NVMe DMA time for MLP chunk → compute-I/O overlap is feasible, validating the prefetch window. No-go: NVMe read is faster than compressed attention GEMV → the prefetch window closes and the strategy degenerates to sequential streaming. Numerical threshold: attention GEMV / NVMe read ratio ≥ 0.8 across layers.

**Primary risk.** ik_llama.cpp on Windows 11 may not support double-buffered NVMe DMA for selective layer streaming — the existing NVMe offload path in llama.cpp is not designed for attention-only DRAM / MLP-only NVMe splits. Mitigation: the correctness of the tier assignment (attention fits in DRAM after compression) can be validated without modifying the inference engine; the I/O overlap optimization is a follow-on. First rung is purely a residency measurement.

## W_down SPECTRUM — W_down Spectrum Closure (W_down-SPEC) — R / Track B

**Mechanism.** Track B. CF8 established W_gate and W_up full-rank; the SUMMARY.md note at Round 4 says W_down "almost certainly applies to W_down too (untested but predictable)." This is a $35-minute measurement that closes the MLP weight-class characterization and has downstream consequences for every remaining compression idea. W_down ∈ R^{d × 4d} (d=2048, 4d=6144 for Qwen3-1.7B) — note it is WIDER than W_gate/W_up (d × 4d vs 4d × d). Its structure may differ: W_down projects from intermediate (high-dim) to residual (low-dim), which is a "bottleneck" projection. Bottleneck projections can have effective rank bounded by d (output dim), not 4d (input dim) — so r_99/d might be < 1 even if W_gate/W_up are at r_99/d ≈ 1.0. This is not speculation: the mathematical argument is that any d × 4d matrix has at most d non-zero singular values, and if the model uses W_down as a true bottleneck, the top-d singular values carry most variance. The Reach case: if W_down is compressible at K=d/2 = 1024, then every MLP block sheds 50% of W_down storage. At 72B with W_down = 4×d×4d per layer = 4×8192×32768×64 layers = 68B params of W_down (at bf16 = 136 GB), a 50% W_down compression saves 68 GB — enough to make DRAM-resident 70B arithmetic close. This is not the "apply SVD to W_up expecting low-rank from CF1" failure mode (CF7/CF8 killed that). W_down has a different structural argument: its output dimension d is a hard ceiling on effective rank, and the question is whether the top-d singular values are concentrated.

**Residency arithmetic.** Qwen3-72B: d=8192, intermediate=28672 (Qwen3-72B uses intermediate_size=28672 not 4×d=32768 — use 28672). W_down ∈ R^{8192 × 28672}. Max singular values: 8192. At IQ4_XS baseline: W_down is 8192×28672×64 × 0.55/8 bytes = 16.3 GB. If r_99/d = 0.6 (W_down has rank ≤ 4915), a K=2048 SVD (25% of max rank) achieves var@K = ? — this depends on the actual spectrum, which is unknown. But: if W_down spectrum looks like W_K (r_99/d ≈ 0.79) rather than W_gate (r_99/d ≈ 1.0), then K=2048 might capture 90% of variance → ΔNLL comparable to W_K compression at K=512 (~+0.29 nats). At K=2048 on 72B W_down: storage = (8192×2048 + 2048×28672)×64 × 0.55/8 ≈ (16.8M + 58.7M) × 64 × 0.069 bytes ≈ 75.5M × 4.4 bytes = 0.33 GB. Total W_down compressed to 0.33 GB vs 16.3 GB uncompressed = 50× savings on W_down at K=2048 at IQ4_XS. Even at K=8192 (full rank): the SVD factored form is identical in storage. The savings come only if K is meaningfully smaller than min(d, d_int). The experiment first measures whether that's true.

**Novelty gloss.** Closest kill-list item: CF8 (MLP weights full-rank) explicitly notes "almost certainly applies to W_down too (untested)." W_down-SPEC is the measurement that closes this explicitly open question, not a variant of a killed idea. Closest published method: standard SVD spectrum analysis of Transformer weight matrices (many papers, none targeting W_down specifically in a post-training context with the question "does the output-dimension bottleneck suppress effective rank?"). The structural novelty is the dimension-asymmetry argument: W_gate/W_up are d_int × d (square-ish) with no bottleneck; W_down is d × d_int with a hard output-rank cap at d. This distinction may flip the spectrum result.

**Smallest experiment.** Testable claim: W_down in Qwen3-1.7B has r_99/d_out (where d_out=2048) ≤ 0.8, indicating its effective rank is bounded by something less than d_out = 2048. Runtime: SVD of a single layer's W_down (2048 × 6144) — seconds. All 28 layers: ~10 min. Go: mean r_99/d_out ≤ 0.8 across layers 5–27 (consistent with W_K; compressible). No-go: mean r_99/d_out ≥ 0.95 (consistent with W_gate/W_up; full-rank bottleneck). Either result closes the MLP characterization: GO opens a new compression path for W_down; NO-GO confirms CF8 generalizes to all three MLP matrices. The numerical gate is r_99/2048 at K=1024 (50% of max rank). CF10 note: no calibration fitting here; this is a pure spectral measurement.

**Primary risk.** The output-dimension-bottleneck argument is a mathematical property of the singular value count, not a spectral concentration argument — W_down may have r_99/d ≈ 1.0 AND all singular values concentrated in the first 2048 components (since it can't have more). Mitigation: measure the full singular value curve; the relevant quantity is var@K for K << d_out, which tests concentration directly.

## AERO-SwiGLU-PROBE — AERO Fold Applicability on SwiGLU Without Retraining (AERO-PROBE) — R / Track A

**Mechanism [FREE SWING].** Track A. AERO (arXiv:2410.13060) was cited in Round 3/4 as "the only known mechanism to truly eliminate W_gate — activation removal + algebraic merge." The SUMMARY.md notes "activation removal hasn't been tested for SwiGLU specifically without retraining." The Reach question is audacious: AERO was designed for models WITH activation removal; can a calibration-derived algebraic fold approximate the SwiGLU gate output distribution tightly enough to eliminate W_gate without retraining, by replacing silu(W_gate·x) with a calibration-fitted approximation g(x) that is expressed in terms of x alone (no W_gate)? The mechanism is: model silu(W_gate·x) ≈ f(W_up·x) (since CF1 says W_up dominates firing rank). If the correlation between silu(W_gate·x) and some function of W_up·x is high enough, g(x) = f_fit(W_up·x) can replace the gate branch without a separate W_gate lookup. This is NOT the R3 CSMF polynomial substitution error (which tried to substitute f(Wx) but still needed W_gate to evaluate Wx). The AERO-PROBE mechanism is: measure the Pearson correlation between silu(W_gate·x) and σ(W_up·x) (or any function of W_up·x) on calibration data. If correlation > 0.95, the gate can be replaced by a function of the up-projection, eliminating W_gate from inference entirely. This is a Track A change (computation graph loses the gate branch). The key to not repeating R3's error: g(x) = f(W_up·x) does NOT require W_gate at inference — it only requires W_up, which was already needed. If the correlation is confirmed, the gate can be hardcoded to a scalar calibration constant (as in AERO layer-1 anomaly — CF6 shows 36% foldable in layer 1). The cascade: (rung 1) measure silu(W_gate·h) correlation with W_up·h on calibration data, per layer; (rung 2) if correlation > 0.95 for any layers, implement the fold for those layers; (rung 3) measure ΔNLL.

**Residency arithmetic.** If even 10% of layers admit the fold (AERO-style): at 72B, W_gate is ≈ 8192×28672×64 = 15B params at bf16 = 30 GB. 10% saving = 3 GB. At IQ4_XS: ~1.6 GB. Small. But CF6 shows Layer 1 has 36% foldable neurons even without activation removal — which means partial fold at Layer 1 is already supported. The Reach case is: if deep layers show high gate-up correlation (because firing-rank is W_up-dominated per CF1), a larger fraction of layers might admit partial folds. The 36% Layer-1 figure (CF6) on 1.7B × 64 layers × 10% = 6.4 layers folded at 36% = 2.3 equivalent layers = 2.3 GB at IQ4_XS on 72B. More importantly: if the fold applies to any layer, the cascade is a no-cost algebraic identity — unlike quantization, it adds no quality-cost from discretization, only from the correlation error.

**Novelty gloss.** Closest kill-list item: R3/S6 CSMF (polynomial SiLU substitution claiming W_gate elimination) — KILLED for math flaw. AERO-PROBE is structurally distinct: it does not attempt polynomial substitution. It measures whether the gate's output correlates with a function of the up-projection's output (a different observable), and only folds the gate if the empirical correlation is confirmed. The math distinguishes: CSMF tried to expand f(Wx) in W_gate's domain; AERO-PROBE checks whether silu(W_gate·x) ≈ h(W_up·x) as a data-driven approximation. CSMF's error was trying to keep W_gate implicit; AERO-PROBE's fold is valid only if W_gate can be fully eliminated (replaced by a function of W_up·x that doesn't require W_gate at inference). Closest published method: AERO itself (arXiv:2410.13060) — but AERO requires activation removal at training time. AERO-PROBE tests whether the same algebraic structure emerges post-hoc in trained Qwen3 without activation removal.

**Smallest experiment.** Testable claim: silu(W_gate·x) and some function of W_up·x have Pearson correlation > 0.9 on calibration data for at least 5 consecutive layers of Qwen3-1.7B. Runtime: forward pass on 200 calibration prompts, collect (silu(W_gate·h), W_up·h) pairs per layer; compute correlation. ~45 min on Ryzen. Go: correlation > 0.9 for ≥ 5 layers → fold is worth attempting. No-go: correlation < 0.7 across all layers → W_gate and W_up branches carry decorrelated information and the fold cannot be calibration-derived without retraining. Even on no-go, the experiment settles whether CF1 (W_up dominates firing RANK) implies any correlation between the TWO BRANCHES in activation SPACE — a distinct and previously unmeasured structural question.

**Primary risk.** Firing-rank dominance (CF1) is a rank-order statistic (top-K neurons); inter-branch correlation is a second-moment statistic. These are independent: W_up can dominate the rank of the product silu(W_gate·x)⊙(W_up·x) without the two factors being correlated. If they are uncorrelated, the fold is inadmissible and the only path to W_gate elimination remains AERO with retraining. Mitigation: the smallest experiment settles this directly in < 1 day.
