## 3. Convergence Map

**Cluster C1 — Cross-layer W_Q subspace coherence.**
*Shared primitive*: whether all 28 (or 64) W_Q matrices across layers share a common dominant right singular subspace of dimension D* << d, derivable from PCA-stacking the W_Q matrices without retraining.
*Contributing orientations*: R-CROSS-Q (Reach: cross-layer basis reduces W_Q storage from L×d² to ~d×K_global + L×K_global×d), C-CQBAL (Composition: couples CF2 residual-stream parallelism with CF11 head-redundancy into a Grassmannian distance claim on adjacent layers' subspaces), F-CQSGC (First-Principles: gauge-collapse derivation showing that a shared right singular space is the gauge-fixed coordinate for the residual stream attention computation), A-GFRS (Constraint-Alien: gauge-rotation constraint forces identification of the W_Q W_K^T joint diagonalizer, which shares the load-bearing question of whether W_Q subspaces are jointly structured across layers).
*Strongest representative*: F-CQSGC (clearest constructive framing, explicit GO threshold, NO-GO finding named).
*Cheapest settler*: randomized SVD of stacked W_Q^(1..28) matrix (57344×2048), measuring var@K for K ∈ {64, 128, 256, 512}. Runtime ~15min. This single number determines advancement for F-CQSGC, R-CROSS-Q, C-CQBAL, and F-CQSMR simultaneously.
*Convergence note*: Four independently generated orientations all point at the same unmeasured structural number — the cross-layer W_Q coherence. This is the strongest cross-orientation signal in this union. The measurement is cheap. If GO (var@128 > 0.80), it opens W_Q compression of 14–32× on 1.7B, scaling to ~7 GB savings at 70B, and is the upstream measurement that gates several other advancers. If NO-GO, it closes the cross-layer W_Q family definitively (all four ideas die simultaneously) and refocuses Stage 3 on per-layer head-shared compression (CF11 already confirmed GO there).

**Cluster C2 — CF3 Jaccard phase transition as three-tier activation-quantization boundary.**
*Shared primitive*: the Jaccard transition from 0.718 (K=0.1%) to 0.308 (K=1%) in CF3 defines a natural tier partition for activation quantization — FP16 for the static top-2 channels, INT8 for the 18-channel dynamic shell, INT4 for the bulk — whose boundaries are derivable from CF3 directly without calibration fitting.
*Contributing orientations*: R-RAOK-70B (Reach: three-tier codebook for 70B cascade), C-RAOK-Grounded (Composition: explicit coupling of CF3 and CF6 into a calibration-free tier-boundary derivation), F-OCSSQ (First-Principles: algebraic derivation of the tier partition from CF3 fixed-point conditions).
*Strongest representative*: C-RAOK-Grounded (best A1, cleanest composition claim with explicit coupling equation; F-OCSSQ advances as convergence rep for the algebraic derivation framing).
*Cheapest settler*: Tier-0 channel stability measurement (top-2 channels by magnitude rank across 200 diverse prompts, ≥90% same-channel stability in ≥20/28 layers — 10min using existing R2 PDAP dataset).
*Convergence note*: CF3 was produced by this pipeline's own R2 PDAP experiment; three orientations independently converged on the same derivation, suggesting CF3's three-tier structure is strongly implied by the data. The Tier-0 stability measurement is the critical upstream gate: if the top-2 channels are not stable across ≥90% of tokens, the FP16 bypass must be demoted to per-token detection, and the INT8 shell must absorb what was Tier-0. The convergence makes this measurement the highest-leverage single number in the Track B union.

**Cluster C3 — Attention (W_Q, W_K) gauge freedom and joint structure.**
*Shared primitive*: the attention score depends only on M = W_Q W_K^T, not on the individual factors — a gauge freedom in the head-coordinate space. Whether W_Q and W_K jointly admit cheaper storage under this invariant (via CCA, joint diagonalization, or operator-norm proximity).
*Contributing orientations*: F-AJDGF (First-Principles: CCA factorization on per-head (W_Q, W_K) pairs), A-GFRS (Constraint-Alien: gauge-rotation constraint that forces identification of joint diagonalizers). Also partially: R-MCQKV and C-ABAR reference this invariant implicitly, though they use independent SVD rather than exploiting the joint gauge.
*Strongest representative*: F-AJDGF (most explicit algebraic identity, strongest A4).
*Cheapest settler*: Frobenius ratio ‖W_Q W_Q^T − W_K W_K^T‖_F / ‖W_Q W_Q^T‖_F for all 28 layers (~10min). If ratio < 0.30 for ≥14 layers (A-GFRS GO condition), joint diagonalization is feasible and CCA compression (F-AJDGF) is motivated. If > 0.50 everywhere, the gauge exploitation gains nothing over independent SVD.

## 4. Frame-Novelty Bonus Advancers

**C-CTERA** (A2=3). The embed-query alignment frame α = ‖E^T P_Q1‖²_F / (‖E‖²_F × ‖P_Q1‖²_F) is a genuinely fresh coupling: it asks whether the tied-embed matrix (CF12: catastrophically full-rank, must stay at high bpw) is ALREADY co-optimized with the first-layer W_Q projection (CF11: concentrated, K=128 compressible). No published compression paper has expressed this as a diagnosable alignment angle. The closest analog is LoftQ's rotation-absorption, but that targets quantization error mitigation, not embed-query alignment diagnosis. The CF12–CF11 coupling (tied-embed rigidity forces high bpw; W_Q concentration creates a bottleneck at the first projection; resolving whether the two are co-optimized or decoupled) is novel enough that no round-specific saturated-frame list entry covers it. A4=2 (elementary matrix product, no theorem import). A3=3 (30-second computation, binary threshold at α ≥ 0.3). Total 12 — would advance under Path 1 as well.

**F-RSCLE** (A2=3). Importing active-subspace methods from numerical uncertainty quantification into the residual-stream representation. Treating the per-layer update covariance C^(L) = E[δ_L δ_L^T] as a "spectral graph Laplacian" of the layer-to-layer residual transition, then using its eigenspectrum to define per-layer active subspaces for cheaper computation, is a frame the ML compression literature has not used. The closest published work (intrinsic dimensionality of neural representations) uses other estimators; the active-subspace method on per-layer update covariance is distinct. No round-specific saturated-frame entry exists for this. However: A1=1 (compute reduction from the active-subspace routing does not translate directly to tok/s in bandwidth-dominated inference; the payoff materializes only when composed with activation quantization). This is a speculative frame with high information value but uncertain payoff — advancing under Path 3 is the correct call, with Stage 3 tasked to determine whether the active-subspace + activation-quantization composition produces a measurable bandwidth win.

Kill-list style: *Attention-tier DRAM / MLP-tier NVMe split — integration narrative; mechanism covered by component ideas MCQKV and CROSS-Q; not an independent primitive.*

A4=1 (the dual-stream AVX2 scheduling argument — 64 bytes/cycle servicing both compressed W_Q and compressed activation fetches simultaneously — is hand-waved; the isomorphism claim "both compress to ~4×" requires measuring both compression ratios and verifying the AVX2 dispatch claim, which is gestured rather than derived). The load-bearing scheduling claim (fetch 64 bytes of compressed W_Q AND fetch 64 bytes of compressed activation in a single VMOVDQU64 stream) conflates memory bandwidth with CPU dispatch width and is not constructive. Surviving primitive: the observation that W_Q and activation-tier compression ratios may be matched (~4×) is a real structural alignment worth noting as a composition hint for Stage 4, but the scheduling mechanism needs to be derived.

- **U-NTOB** — REJECT-low-score (total 8, A4=1, A1=1). The sector-alignment benefit (~3% at QD=1) is too small for the Path 1 threshold given A4=1. A4=1 because the primary mechanism (split-sector read overhead under NVMe QD=1) is cited plausibly but the actual overhead estimate (350 misaligned reads × 100µs = 35ms/token, 3% of baseline) is not independently auditable — the 100µs per-stall assumption is a range, not a measured number. The kill from R1/S2 ("OS-Paging-Aware Weight Layout") correctly noted that the Cache Manager may mask misalignment; the primary-risk acknowledgment here does not resolve this. Kill-list style: *NTOB — small gain (~3% at QD=1) contingent on unverified per-stall cost; mechanism superseded by AQPT if Windows Cache Manager QD question is resolved first.*

- **U-ZDPW** — REJECT-low-score (total 9). A1=1 (17% speedup assumes metadata is the bottleneck and 4× compression holds simultaneously; neither is confirmed). A4=2 (zstd dictionary compression is a well-understood primitive; no broken theorem import). Falls below Path 1 threshold; A1 claim is too contingent. Kill-list style: *ZDPW — 17% speedup claim requires both metadata-bottleneck confirmation AND 4× dictionary-compression ratio; primary-risk (multimodal metadata distribution) may limit to 1.5× compression; kill if compression ratio < 2× on smallest experiment.*

- **U-VLAP** — REJECT-low-score (total 9). A1=1 (the 20% NVMe-load reduction from eliminating attention reads is real but modest; the primary bottleneck remains MLP streaming). A4=2. A5=2. The primary blocker is VirtualLock privilege gating on Windows 11 Home (acknowledged in the primary-risk section); the mechanism is conditioned on a permission that may not be grantable. Also: VLAP's attention-pinning benefit is largely superseded by ATTN-NVMe-SPLIT and MCQKV combined. Kill-list style: *VLAP — primary value absorbed by attention-compression ideas (MCQKV, CROSS-Q); VirtualLock privilege uncertainty is a deployment blocker; advances only as a systems hygiene optimization once the attention-compression rung is confirmed.*

- **U-BPAT** — REJECT-low-score (total 8, A1=0). Branch-predictor alignment of GGUF block-type dispatch. A1=0: the mechanism (11ms/pass in misprediction cost reduction) is a <0.1% throughput improvement, with no residency effect and no quality effect. Below A1=1 even on A1 definition (no 1.2× floor approach). Kill-list style: *BPAT — <0.1% throughput improvement; below A1 floor; engineering micro-optimization with no structural finding value.*

- **U-CAWB** — REJECT-CF9 (A4=1). This is the content-addressable deduplication by BLAKE3-hash of INT8-quantized SVD action signatures. CF9 note: the deduplication claim implicitly assumes that two shards with similar singular vectors will hash to the same BLAKE3 key (the "action key" is the INT8-quantized top-64 singular vectors). But BLAKE3 is a cryptographic hash — it is designed to produce distinct outputs for any two inputs with probability (1 - 2^{-256}); two shards with cosine similarity 0.85 in their singular vectors will have entirely different BLAKE3 hashes unless their bit representations are identical. The mechanism as described conflates "similar action" with "identical hash," which breaks the content-addressable property. After removing the broken hash-based dedup, what remains is: measure cosine similarity between W_Q head singular vectors (CF11 head-redundancy measurement) and store near-duplicate heads once. This is a valid dedup, but the mechanism is now direct cosine-threshold deduplication, not content-addressable hashing — the BLAKE3 framing is vestigial. *Stripped-primitive note: the surviving structure after removing the broken BLAKE3 framing is threshold-based W_Q head dedup from measured cosine similarity; Stage 4 may want to attack this from an orientation that exploits the CF11 head-redundancy measurement directly (e.g., Composition or First-Principles) without the content-addressable scaffolding.*

- **A-TSWD** — REJECT-low-score (total 9). A4=1 because the critical mechanism step — "tropical argmax gate on W_up inner-products selects K=20% neurons that produce acceptable quality" — depends on the Qwen3 MLP being functionally sparse at K=20%, which CF1 does not guarantee. CF1 says W_up·x dominates firing RANK, not that 80% of neurons can be dropped without quality loss. SABLE-v1 was killed for exactly this diffuseness failure mode. The mechanism is checkable (smallest experiment proposed), but A4=1 because the theoretical connection from CF1 to K=20% quality tolerance is hand-waved, not derived. A5=2 because the tropical-semiring framing is genuinely novel in vocabulary. Kill-list style: *TSWD — A4=1; CF1 firing-rank dominance does NOT imply K=20% sparsity tolerance; effective sparsity must be measured before the tropical gate has a mechanism; if sparsity > 50% achieves ΔNLL < 0.5 nats, promote to Stage 3.*

- **A-CAWS** — REJECT-low-score (total 9). A4=1 because the collision mechanism (two shards with similar top-64 singular vectors hash to the same BLAKE3 key) has the same CF9-adjacent flaw as U-CAWB: BLAKE3 hashes will almost certainly be distinct even for shards with cosine similarity 0.85, unless the INT8 quantization produces bit-identical vectors (which requires cosine similarity essentially = 1.0). A4 is 1 rather than 0 because the idea can be rescued by interpreting "content-addressable" as "threshold-cosine-dedup" rather than actual hash collision — the mechanism survives if the experiment is reframed as "find W_Q head pairs with cos similarity > 0.85 and store them once." But as written, the BLAKE3 hash step is mechanistically broken for the proposed use. Kill-list style: *CAWS — BLAKE3 cannot produce collisions for cos-similar (non-bit-identical) shards; mechanism requires reframing to threshold-cosine-dedup; survives if reframed (see A4 note). Merged with CF11 head-dedup path.*

- **A-ALSA** — REJECT-low-score (total 9). A1=1 (payoff is KV access pattern optimization for 32K+ context; at 4K context there is no residency reduction and no throughput benefit). A5=2 (log-structured KV on NVMe is a fresh frame). However, total 9 falls short of Path 1 threshold given A1 weakness. A4=2: the mechanism is sound (append-only flat file + mmap is a real primitive). Primary limitation: at the target hardware's 4K context operating regime, KV fits in DRAM entirely; ALSA's value materializes only at 32K+, which is not the primary deployment scenario. Kill-list style: *ALSA — value conditional on 32K+ context; at 4K context, KV fits in DRAM and sequential NVMe advantage is moot; revisit when 32K+ context is the target use case.*

- **A-RORS** — REJECT-low-score (total 9). A1=1 (1.2 tok/s at 7B on purely NVMe path is below the 3 tok/s target; the constraint-imposed NVMe-direct path is slower than the current llama.cpp DRAM path for models that fit in RAM). A4=2 (FILE_FLAG_NO_BUFFERING is a real Windows primitive; the arithmetic is checkable). The "no DRAM" constraint is too strong — the payoff from eliminating DRAM (zero-DRAM inference at 70B) does not close because the NVMe streaming rate alone cannot reach the tok/s floor. Kill-list style: *RORS — no-DRAM NVMe streaming achieves ~1.2 tok/s at 7B, below the 3 tok/s target; value is as an engineering curiosity (true zero-DRAM floor measurement) not a deployment path; keep as a calibration measurement for NVMe bandwidth floor.*

- **A-FBVS** — REJECT-CF9 (A4=1). The FSM-bounded vocabulary decoder uses the Myhill-Nerode theorem as the theoretical frame. CF9 check: Myhill-Nerode requires that the language has a finite number of equivalence classes under the right-congruence relation. For free natural language token sequences from a 151936-vocabulary model, the number of equivalence classes is not finite (the transformer's output distribution at each step depends on the full context, not just the last 1-3 tokens). The Myhill-Nerode precondition does not hold for the general-purpose LLM output distribution. The proposed mechanism (train a 512-state FSM from bigram calibration data) is a valid engineering heuristic, but the Myhill-Nerode framing that motivates it (minimizing the FSM to the "correct" number of states) is inapplicable. After stripping the Myhill-Nerode import, what remains is: "use a bigram prediction table as a fast approximation to the full transformer, with fallback." This is an n-gram language model with transformer fallback — a well-known prior art approach. *Stripped-primitive note: the surviving structure is an n-gram prefix cache with transformer fallback; Stage 4 may want to attack n-gram caching from the substrate orientation (exact bigram matching is content-addressable lookup, which the U orientation covers), noting that the bigram table is 78 MB and fits in DRAM — a valid engineering optimization even without the Myhill-Nerode framing.*
