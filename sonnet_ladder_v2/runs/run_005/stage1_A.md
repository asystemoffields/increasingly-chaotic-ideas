## A1-GFRS-2: Gauge-Fixed Residual Stream — Channel Sign-Flip Collapsed Codebook

**Constraint:** The computation graph must algebraically simplify under channel sign-flip symmetry of the residual stream.

**Track B.** For any residual stream channel $i$, the trained network has a family of equivalent models obtained by flipping the sign of all weights that read or write channel $i$ simultaneously: $W_{\text{in},*i} \to -W_{\text{in},*i}$, $W_{\text{out},i*} \to -W_{\text{out},i*}$. This is a discrete Z_2^d gauge symmetry of the residual stream (not an attention-subspace claim — no W_Q subspace stacking). The trained network has been gradient-updated without any gauge-fixing, so the d=2048 sign bits are arbitrary degrees of freedom that carry zero information. Fix gauge by requiring $e_i^T W_{\text{out},\text{layer1}} \geq 0$ for every channel $i$ (make the first-layer output projection non-negative row-wise). Then re-sign all downstream weight rows accordingly: $O(L \times d \times d_{\text{head}})$ sign operations, no matmul, no retraining. After gauge-fixing, the sign pattern of every weight matrix is "almost non-negative" — or at minimum has a structured sign partition. A 1-bit sign codebook can then separate the integer-magnitude from the sign, halving codebook complexity at any bpw. Constraint difficulty: eliminates any scheme that doesn't respect gauge-covariance (~60% of standard quantization schemes ignore sign structure entirely); forces codebook design to be sign-aware.

**Residency arithmetic.** Working at 4B (IQ4_XS, ~2.1 GiB). The sign-codebook layer reduces 4-bit magnitude/sign-entangled quantization to 3-bit magnitude + 1-bit sign plane stored separately. If the sign plane compresses well (e.g., ~0.5 bit/weight via RLE on the gauge-fixed, nearly-monotone sign structure), effective bpw drops from 4.0 to ~3.5. 4B at 3.5 bpw = ~1.84 GiB, saving ~0.26 GiB. At DRAM bandwidth 11.5 GB/s: 1.84 GiB / 11.5 GB/s × overhead ~ 0.16 s/token → ~6 tok/s vs ~5 tok/s at 4 bpw. Modest but free (no quality cost if gauge-fixing is exact). Sign entropy measurement is the key unknown.

**Novelty gloss.** Kill list closest: v2-CHEAP-TEST-001 is cross-layer W_Q stacking — different axis entirely; this is within-layer sign symmetry of the residual stream. Closest published: SmoothQuant migrates outliers across layers; LLM.int8() separates outlier channels. Neither applies a gauge-fixing argument or exploits the Z_2^d residual-stream symmetry to compress the sign representation. The constraint forces a move the literature has no vocabulary for: fixing gauge to make quantization coordinate-free.

**Smallest experiment.** Claim: after Z_2-gauge-fixing on Qwen3-1.7B-Base, the sign entropy of W_up (all layers, all rows) drops to < 0.8 bits/weight. Load W_up layer 0 in bf16; compute per-row sign pattern before and after row-sign normalization (flip each row so its max-abs element is positive); measure empirical H(sign) per row. Runtime: <10 min on Ryzen. Go threshold: mean H(sign) after normalization < 0.8 bits/weight. NO-GO structural finding: sign patterns are random-looking even in gauge-fixed coordinates → quantization codebook design should be sign-agnostic.

**Primary risk.** After gauge-fixing, H(sign) remains ~1.0 bit (signs are effectively iid Bernoulli), making the sign plane incompressible. Mitigation: verify claim cheaply on a single layer before any codebook engineering; even partial compression (H < 0.9) on deep layers (where CF3 shows more channel diversity) would motivate a hybrid scheme.

## A2-TROP: Tropical Semiring Attention Approximation

**Constraint:** No continuous arithmetic during the attention score computation — only max and addition (tropical algebra).

**Track A.** Standard softmax attention computes $A_{ij} = \exp(q_i \cdot k_j / \sqrt{d})$ and normalizes. Replace the softmax with a tropical (max-plus) operation: $\hat{A}_{ij} = [q_i \cdot k_j = \max_j q_i \cdot k_j]$ — a hard argmax over keys, dispatching each query to its single nearest key. This is well-studied for linear attention approximations (Performer, Cosformer) but none of them imposes the tropical constraint as a hard binding. Constraint difficulty: eliminates all continuous weighting schemes, all softmax variants, all linear-attention random-feature approximations (~80% of published attention mechanisms). Forces a pure routing view: attention becomes a content-addressable lookup where each query retrieves exactly one key-value pair (or k-nearest under a max-heap). Computation graph changes: softmax GEMM (O(n^2 d)) → top-1 nearest-neighbor retrieval (O(n log n) with a FAISS-style IVF flat index over K vectors, or O(n d) sequential scan). For the 4K context regime on CPU, sequential scan is ~670 MB × 28 layers / 11.5 GB/s = 1.6 s — slower than matmul, so the win only emerges if the IVF index compresses KV. Residency math: 4K context × 28 layers × 2 (K+V) × 2048 × bf16 = 0.46 GiB KV; tropical dispatch eliminates softmax numerics but doesn't reduce KV bytes directly. The real win: tropical attention allows *integer keys* — once you commit to hard argmax, K vectors can be int8-quantized without precision loss in the decision boundary, saving 2× on KV bandwidth.

**Residency arithmetic.** KV at 4K context: 0.46 GiB at bf16 → 0.23 GiB at int8 K (V stays bf16 for output quality). Net KV saving: ~0.23 GiB. Not a residency revolution, but the constraint forces a clean int8-key inference path with no approximation error in the dispatch decision (argmax is invariant to affine scaling). Token throughput: KV bandwidth halved for K retrieval; at 4K context KV is not the bottleneck vs weights anyway. Stronger case at 32K+ context.

**Novelty gloss.** Kill list: no tropical attention entry. Published landscape: Mamba / state-space models replace softmax but not with tropical algebra; Reformer uses LSH (probabilistic, not tropical). Tropical attention as a hard constraint has no named entry in the 2024-2026 compression literature — it's a reframing from max-plus semiring theory applied to the query-key scoring step. The non-obvious move: tropical algebra makes key quantization *lossless* for routing purposes, which no softmax-preserving method can claim.

**Smallest experiment.** Claim: on Qwen3-1.7B-Base, top-1 tropical attention (hard argmax over K) achieves PPL within 0.3 nats of full softmax on WikiText-2. Load model; replace attention softmax with argmax (zero temperature limit); evaluate 512-token PPL. Runtime: ~30 min (CPU forward pass, no optimization). Go threshold: ΔNLL < 0.3 nats. NO-GO: hard argmax attention is catastrophically lossier than softmax, bounding tropical schemes to k>1 nearest-neighbor (k-tropical) which requires a separate feasibility experiment.

**Primary risk.** Hard argmax destroys the distributed weighting that makes attention expressible — PPL collapse on NO-GO. Mitigation: test k=1 tropical first; if NO-GO, test k=4 tropical (top-4 hard uniform weighting) as a midpoint before abandoning the constraint.

## A3-SEED: 10 MB Seed Weight Synthesis via Spectral Generator

**Constraint:** The model must be reconstructible from a 10 MB seed — all weights derivable from a small generative procedure.

**Track A.** A 1.7B model at bf16 is ~3.4 GB. Kolmogorov complexity intuition: if the trained weights were low-complexity objects, a short description would suffice. They are not (CF4, CF5, CF8 show full-rank). The constraint forces a different angle: not "the weights are compressible" but "the weights are a deterministic function of a seed via a fast generator." Concretely: represent each weight matrix $W_L$ as $f(\theta_{\text{seed}}, L, \text{type})$ where $f$ is a small MLP / Fourier series / hypernetwork evaluated at layer index. The seed $\theta_{\text{seed}}$ (≤10 MB = ~5M float16 parameters) must produce all 1.7B weights on demand per token via streaming synthesis. Constraint difficulty: eliminates all stored-weight schemes, all quantization codebooks stored at inference time, essentially all prior compression (~95% inadmissible). Forces a generative / procedural model — the computation graph grows a synthesis step before each matmul. Stack primitive: the Ryzen's L3 cache (16 MB) can hold the seed; the synthesis function runs in registers/L1/L2 per layer, discarding generated weights after use (no RAM storage of weights at all during inference). This is the "no RAM for weights" constraint relaxed to "synthesize on demand from L3-resident seed."

**Residency arithmetic.** Seed in L3: 10 MB. Weight RAM: 0 (generated and discarded per layer). Effective residency: 10 MB. At 6.2 tok/s reference, the synthesis compute overhead is the cost: if synthesis of one W_up layer (8M parameters) takes T_synth, and matmul with input takes T_mm, throughput = 1/(T_synth + T_mm). If T_synth << T_mm (synthesis is cheap, e.g., Hadamard product of seed sub-tensor), this is viable. T_synth budget: T_mm for one layer ≈ 2 × 2048 × 6144 FMAs / (Ryzen AVX2 throughput ~64 GFLOP/s) ≈ 1.6 ms → T_synth must be < 0.5 ms for 3× overhead. Feasibility is load-bearing unknown.

**Novelty gloss.** Kill list: no seed-synthesis entry. Published landscape: hypernetworks (Ha et al. 2016) generate weights at train-time, not inference-time; SMASH (Brock et al. 2018) is NAS, not deployment; HyperDiffusion generates model parameters from diffusion noise. None operates as an inference-time synthesis constraint on CPU. The constraint-binding move: seed in L3, zero RAM for weights, synthesis per layer. This has no named entry in LLM deployment literature.

**Smallest experiment.** Claim: a 10 MB seed network (MLP with 5M params) can synthesize the singular vectors of W_up layer 0 with reconstruction error (Frobenius) < 5% of ‖W_up‖_F. Sample a random 10 MB MLP; condition on (layer_index=0, row_index, col_index) → predict weight value; train on W_up layer 0 only (no gradient through the base model). Runtime: ~2 hr CPU training on one layer. Go threshold: test-set Frobenius residual < 5%. NO-GO: seed-synthesis requires more than 10 MB to represent even one layer → bound on Kolmogorov complexity of trained weights, itself a structural finding.

**Primary risk.** Full-rank weights (CF4, CF5, CF8) imply high Kolmogorov complexity — a 10 MB seed cannot represent 3.4 GB of high-entropy weights. Mitigation: reframe as *lossy* synthesis targeting ΔNLL < 0.5 nats, not exact reconstruction; the experiment tests the lossy rate-distortion point, not exact recovery.

## A4-APND: Append-Only Inference — Log-Structured KV with Monotonic Residual

**Constraint:** All state is append-only during inference — no in-place mutation of any buffer.

**Track A.** Standard autoregressive inference mutates the KV cache in place (append new K,V; overwrite previous attention outputs). Under the append-only constraint: the KV cache is a log — each new K_t, V_t appends to an immutable ring buffer, and attention at step t is computed over the full prefix log without any masking mutation. Residual stream updates are also append-only: instead of h_{L+1} ← h_L + Δ_L, maintain a list [h_0, Δ_1, Δ_2, ..., Δ_L] and compute the final residual as a streaming sum only at the output layer. Constraint difficulty: rules out any in-place fused kernel, any GPU-style write-back scheme, essentially all existing KV cache management libraries (~70% inadmissible). Forces: (a) log-structured storage that is naturally aligned to NVMe sequential write (which is 3-4× faster than random write on Windows 11 NVMe), (b) incremental residual stream that can be snapshotted at any layer for early-exit without recomputing, (c) delta compression across the log — consecutive Δ_L are correlated (residual stream changes slowly per CF2: cos(h_L, h_{L+1}) ~ 0.99), so append-only deltas compress aggressively with a simple XOR / int16 diff. Stack primitives: FILE_FLAG_NO_BUFFERING sequential append on NVMe; Windows FILE_APPEND_DATA sharing mode; CPU accumulator register for streaming sum.

**Residency arithmetic.** Append-only residual: instead of one 2048-dim bf16 vector per layer in RAM (28 × 2048 × 2 = 112 KB per token), store 28 deltas in int16 (delta compression of ~0.99-correlated vectors reduces effective range; int16 should hold ± half the delta with negligible error). 28 × 2048 × 2 (int16) = 112 KB same size — no saving on state size. The win is on NVMe write throughput when logging to disk: sequential NVMe write at ~1.5 GB/s vs random write ~300 MB/s → 5× on the I/O path. For CPU-only DRAM-resident inference, append-only residuals are a structural simplification without residency savings, but they enable: (1) zero-copy early exit (snapshot at any layer = a pointer into the log), (2) content-addressable token state (hash the delta sequence = a fingerprint for duplicate detection / KV reuse).

**Novelty gloss.** Kill list: CLASE (cross-layer KV aliasing) was deferred for bottleneck mismatch at short context — different mechanism; CLASE mutates KV, this forbids mutation entirely. Published: RetNet uses recurrent state but mutates it; RWKV is recurrent; none imposes append-only as a hard constraint. Log-structured ML inference has no named entry — the constraint is borrowed from log-structured merge-tree databases (LevelDB, RocksDB) applied to the attention KV and residual stream. The non-obvious move: append-only residuals are naturally delta-compressed because CF2 (cos(h_L, h_{L+1}) ~ 0.99) guarantees small deltas.

**Smallest experiment.** Claim: on Qwen3-1.7B-Base, consecutive-layer residual deltas Δ_L = h_{L+1} - h_L have ‖Δ_L‖_2 / ‖h_L‖_2 < 0.15 on average (confirming int16 delta compression is lossless at 16-bit range). Extract hidden states for 50 tokens across all 28 layers; compute ‖Δ_L‖ / ‖h_L‖. Runtime: ~15 min. Go threshold: mean ratio < 0.15 over all layers. NO-GO structural finding: deltas are large relative to state → residual stream is NOT slowly-varying → int16 delta compression fails, and CF2 (cos ~0.99) does not imply small absolute delta magnitude.

**Primary risk.** CF2 (high cosine similarity) does NOT imply small L2 delta: two vectors can be nearly parallel but both large, with Δ_L large in absolute terms though small as an angle. If ‖Δ_L‖ is large, int16 delta compression loses precision. Mitigation: the experiment directly tests this; if the delta ratio is large, fall back to log-structured KV (which still benefits from sequential NVMe write speed) without residual delta compression.

## A5-FSMI: Finite-State Machine Inference — Discretized Hidden-State Decoder [FREE SWING]

**Constraint:** The inference loop must operate as a finite-state machine of N ≤ 65536 states — hidden state is a discrete index, not a continuous vector.

**Track A.** A transformer's residual stream is 2048-dimensional bf16 — a continuous object. Under the FSM constraint, reduce the hidden state to a discrete index in {0, ..., N-1} where N = 65536 = 2^16. Each layer transition is a function f_L: {0,...,N-1} → {0,...,N-1} implemented as a lookup table (one uint16 array of N entries per layer). The vocabulary mapping is a separate table: for each state index and each of the top-K output tokens, store the logit rank. Stack primitive: a 28-layer FSM has 28 × 65536 × 2 bytes = 3.5 MB of transition tables — fits entirely in L3 cache (16 MB). Inference: no matmul; no DRAM weight fetch; each token is a table lookup in L3. If the calibration distribution fits in 65536 states (a massive if), this is 2× L3-bandwidth-bound rather than DRAM-bandwidth-bound. Constraint difficulty: eliminates all vector arithmetic, all neural network primitives, all continuous representations (~99% inadmissible). The mechanism is pure combinatorics — Myhill-Nerode minimization of the effective transformer states. The question is whether the trained transformer has far fewer than 2^{2048} distinguishable hidden-state behaviors — if it does, a small FSM approximates it.

**Residency arithmetic.** Tables: 3.5 MB in L3. DRAM weight fetch: 0 bytes per token during steady-state inference. L3 bandwidth: ~50 GB/s (Ryzen 5 7530U). Each token = 28 table lookups (28 × 1 uint16 read = 56 bytes). At 50 GB/s L3 bandwidth: 50 GB/s / 56 B/token = ~900M tok/s theoretical. Real bottleneck will be token-ID encoding and cache-line thrash; realistic estimate: ~100 tok/s if tables are L3-hot and lookups are cache-friendly. Catastrophic quality degradation is near-certain at N=65536 — the question is whether a useful subset of the model's behaviors (e.g., template completion for common patterns) is FSM-approximable.

**Novelty gloss.** Kill list: no FSM inference entry anywhere. Published landscape: Mamba / state-space models have a finite hidden state but it's continuous; HMMs are discrete but not transformer-derived; RWKV has discretized variants but not FSM-constraint-bound. The combination of Myhill-Nerode FSM minimization + calibration-derived state assignment + L3-table inference has no named entry in LLM deployment literature. The free-swing claim: if even a small useful subset of LLM inference is FSM-approximable (e.g., common N-gram continuations), the L3-speed win is extreme.

**Smallest experiment.** Claim: on Qwen3-1.7B-Base, the 28-layer residual-stream trajectory for 100 common short prompts (e.g., "The capital of France is") has at most 65536 distinct trajectory prefixes when quantized to int8 per dimension and hashed. Extract all layer-0 hidden states for 100 prompts; quantize each to int8; compute SHA-256 → map to state index; count distinct states. Runtime: <20 min. Go threshold: fewer than 65536 distinct int8-quantized states across all 100 prompts × 28 layers. NO-GO structural finding: trajectory space is effectively continuous even for common prompts → FSM approximation requires N >> 65536 → the constraint is too tight at N=65536 but the correct N (if finite and manageable) is itself a structural finding.

**Primary risk.** Residual-stream dimensionality (d=2048) combined with full-rank weights (CF4, CF5, CF8) means the effective state space is exponentially large — 65536 states is almost certainly insufficient for general inference. Mitigation: bound scope to template-completion tasks where token diversity is low; even a 65536-state template machine for common patterns has deployment value as a speculative-decode draft stage.

## A6-CNTR: Content-Addressable Weight Dispatch — No Spatial Layout

**Constraint:** Every weight must be content-addressable by its action — no spatial layout; weights retrieved by hash of their column input fingerprint.

**Track B.** In a standard GEMV $y = Wx$, rows of $W$ are accessed sequentially by row index. Under the content-addressable constraint, each row $W_i$ is stored at address $\text{BLAKE3}(W_i)$ — retrieved only when the input $x$ matches a fingerprint indicating row $i$ is needed. This forces an action-keyed dispatch: "which rows of $W$ act non-negligibly on input $x$?" is answered by a small index structure over $\text{BLAKE3}(W_i)$. Constraint difficulty: eliminates all spatial-layout weight access patterns, all matrix-layout-optimized kernels (~75% inadmissible). Forces: outlier-aware sparse dispatch — the CF3 K=1% outlier dynamicity finding says ~20 channels of the residual stream are token-dynamic outliers; these 20 channels are the "routing key" for which W_up rows fire most. Build a content-addressed index: for each W_up row, precompute its projection onto the K=1% outlier-channel subspace (~20 dims); hash the resulting 20-dim signature → store in a BLAKE3-keyed skip list. At inference: project input $x$ onto the outlier-channel subspace; hash the signature; retrieve the matching W_up rows. Rows with no matching signature are skipped (zero contribution assumed). This is NOT a low-rank decomposition of W_up (CF8/CF5 kills); it is a content-addressed dispatch using the outlier-channel routing key established by CF3.

**Residency arithmetic.** If the K=1% outlier-channel signature predicts the top-20% of W_up rows (hypothetical; to be verified), then 80% of W_up rows are skipped. W_up per layer: 2048 × 6144 × 2 bytes = 24 MB. Skip 80%: load only 4.8 MB / layer. At DRAM 11.5 GB/s: 4.8 MB / 11.5 GB/s ≈ 0.42 ms/layer vs 2.1 ms/layer full. 28 layers: 11.6 ms/token vs 58 ms/token → 5× throughput on W_up. W_gate and W_down still full: real speedup ~2×. The skip fraction is the key unknown; the CF3 outlier-dynamicity finding provides the routing key but doesn't predict skip rate.

**Novelty gloss.** Kill list: RAOK (R2-aware outlier-keyed activation codebook, Track B R6 deferred) is the closest entry — RAOK is quantization-based; this is dispatch/skip-based with content-addressable indexing. CLUBS/RSTD (cross-layer Tucker on W_up) — dead; this does not touch W_up rank. Published: Deja Vu (arXiv:2310.17157) predicts ReLU neuron firing from attention outputs; SeerAttention sparsifies attention. Content-addressable weight dispatch keyed by CF3 outlier-channel signatures has no named entry. The BLAKE3 dispatch is the novel framing; the CF3 routing key is the empirical anchor.

**Smallest experiment.** Claim: the K=1% outlier-channel projection of $x$ (top 20 channels by abs magnitude) predicts the top-20% of W_up firing rows with precision > 0.50 (vs random baseline 0.20). Extract W_up for layer 0; for 200 calibration tokens, compute outlier-channel signature (top-20 channel activations of residual stream at layer input); check whether rows of W_up with highest projection onto those 20 channels also have highest $(W_{\text{up}} x)_i$ magnitudes. Runtime: ~20 min. Go threshold: precision@20% > 0.50 on held-out tokens. NO-GO: CF3 outlier channels are NOT predictive routing keys for W_up firing → dispatch requires full input $x$, not a 20-dim summary → content-addressable dispatch at this key granularity fails; structural finding: firing prediction requires more than the outlier-channel subspace.

**Primary risk.** CF1 already showed that W_up firing is not easily predicted from residual-stream signals (the SwiGLU interaction makes it complex); content-addressable dispatch at K=1% outlier signature may have the same failure mode as the row-herding predictor from R1. Mitigation: the smallest experiment directly tests the coupling; if precision is only 0.35 (above random but below threshold), the routing key needs to be wider (K=5% outlier channels = 100 dims), which is still a 20× compression of the input.

## A7-NRAM: No-RAM Weight Streaming — NVMe as the Only Weight Store [OUT-OF-ORIENTATION]

**Constraint:** No RAM during decode — only registers, L1/L2/L3, NVMe. Weights never reside in DRAM.

**Track B.** This constraint forces streaming computation: for each layer, stream the layer's weights from NVMe into L3 (16 MB), perform the GEMV entirely in L2/L1 on the streaming chunk, then evict before the next layer. NVMe bandwidth: ~3 GB/s (sequential, extent-prefetch-engaged — CF13* unverified) or ~700 MB/s (random). L3 is 16 MB; W_up for one layer is 24 MB — doesn't fit in L3. Must stream in 16 MB chunks: two NVMe reads per W_up layer. At 3 GB/s NVMe: 24 MB / 3 GB/s = 8 ms per layer for W_up alone; 28 layers × (W_gate + W_up + W_down + attn weights) ≈ 5 × 24 MB × 28 = 3.4 GB total weights → 3.4 GB / 3 GB/s = ~1.1 s/token at 3 GB/s = ~0.9 tok/s. At 700 MB/s (unverified prefetch off): 4.7 s/token. This is below the 3 tok/s floor for usable throughput; the constraint is too tight for full 1.7B inference without prefetch. Useful case: apply the constraint to a sub-model (0.6B, ~1.2 GB weights) which fits in L3 in 75 chunks. At 3 GB/s: 1.2 GB / 3 GB/s = 0.4 s/token = 2.5 tok/s — marginal but close to floor. This idea is tagged OUT-OF-ORIENTATION because it maps closely to substrate-primitive work (Orientation U) rather than a constraint-forcing-a-new-mechanism; the constraint simply instantiates the standard NVMe-offload problem without forcing a new frame. Included for completeness as the convergence signal with Orientation U's natural territory.

**Residency arithmetic.** 0 bytes DRAM for weights; 16 MB L3 working set; NVMe as weight store. Qwen3-0.6B (~1.2 GB): ~0.4 s/token at 3 GB/s NVMe sequential = 2.5 tok/s. Qwen3-1.7B (~3.4 GB): ~1.1 s/token = 0.9 tok/s. Only 0.6B is above the usable floor under this constraint. NOTE: CF13* (NVMe mmap latency numbers) is unverified in v2; re-derivation required if NVMe bandwidth numbers are load-bearing.

**Novelty gloss.** Kill list: no direct entry but the Apple LLM-in-a-flash paper (Wu et al. 2024) is the closest published method — NVMe streaming of weights with selective loading. This idea differs only in imposing zero DRAM for weights as a hard constraint (Apple's paper uses DRAM as a staging buffer). Orientation note: this is more naturally Orientation U than Orientation A; the constraint doesn't force a new mechanism beyond what the substrate-primitive framing already motivates.

**Smallest experiment.** Claim: on the Ryzen 5 7530U, sequential NVMe read bandwidth for a 24 MB file using FILE_FLAG_NO_BUFFERING is > 1.5 GB/s. Read a 24 MB file from NVMe sequentially in 4 MB chunks using Windows ReadFile with FILE_FLAG_NO_BUFFERING; measure wall-clock bandwidth. Runtime: 5 min. Go threshold: > 1.5 GB/s. NO-GO: NVMe bandwidth under Windows unbuffered I/O is < 1.5 GB/s → zero-DRAM streaming is too slow for usable inference → constraint forces a dead-end → structural finding: RAM buffering is necessary for >1 tok/s at 1.7B scale.

**Primary risk.** CF13* (NVMe latency / bandwidth) is unverified in v2; the entire residency case depends on it. Mitigation: the smallest experiment RE-DERIVES the NVMe bandwidth measurement on this hardware, satisfying the CF13* re-derivation requirement. This is the first cascade rung regardless.

## Convergence handles

- **Z_2^d residual-stream gauge symmetry** — sign-flip invariance exploitable by codebook design (A1-GFRS-2)
- **CF3 K=1% outlier-channel identity as routing / dispatch key** — coupled to content-addressed weight skip and to RAOK (deferred Track B R6)
- **NVMe sequential write bandwidth vs random write bandwidth** — load-bearing for A4-APND and A7-NRAM; overlaps Orientation U territory; CF13* re-derivation needed
- **Consecutive-layer residual delta magnitude** — CF2 gives angle but not L2 magnitude; needed for A4-APND delta compression; likely needed by Composition (C) and First-Principles (F) orientations for residual-stream structure
- **Myhill-Nerode / effective-state-space cardinality of the Qwen3 residual stream** — A5-FSMI load-bearing unknown; relevant to any orientation proposing discrete-state approximations
- **Synthesis rate of a small generator network on one weight layer** — A3-SEED feasibility bottleneck; no current CF measurement; cross-orientation if Reach (R) attempts a procedural generation cascade
